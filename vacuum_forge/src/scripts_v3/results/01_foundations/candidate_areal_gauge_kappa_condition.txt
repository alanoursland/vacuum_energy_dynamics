Case 0: General spherical metric in arbitrary radial coordinate
========================================================================================================
General static spherical metric in arbitrary radial coordinate R:
  ds² = -T(R)c²dt² + Q(R)dR² + S(R)² dΩ²

Here:
  T(R) is the temporal coefficient.
  Q(R) is the radial coefficient.
  S(R) is the geometric sphere-radius function.

Sphere area:
  Area(R) = 4π S(R)²

Areal radius:
  r_areal = sqrt(Area/4π) = S(R)

[PASS] arbitrary radial coordinate includes angular-radius function S(R)

Case 1: Areal radius from sphere area
========================================================================================================
Area(R) = 4*pi*S(R)**2
r_areal = sqrt(Area/4π) = sqrt(S(R)**2)

Assuming positive sphere-radius function S(R)>0:
  r_areal = S(R)
[PASS] areal radius is geometrically fixed by sphere area

========================================================================================================
Case 2: Transform arbitrary R to areal radius r=S(R)
========================================================================================================
Define:
  r = S(R)
  dr/dR = S'(R)
  dR/dr = 1/S'(R)

Then in areal gauge:
  A_areal = T(R)
  B_areal = Q(R)/Derivative(S(R), R)**2
  angular sector = r²dΩ²

[PASS] areal radial coefficient includes inverse sphere-radius Jacobian

Case 3: Areal-gauge kappa from arbitrary-coordinate metric
========================================================================================================
kappa_naive_R = 1/2 ln(T Q) = log(Q(R)*T(R))/2
kappa_areal   = 1/2 ln(T Q / S'^2) = log(Q(R)*T(R)/Derivative(S(R), R)**2)/2
kappa_naive_R - kappa_areal = log(Q(R)*T(R))/2 - log(Q(R)*T(R)/Derivative(S(R), R)**2)/2

  kappa_naive_R = kappa_areal + ln S'(R)

[PASS] difference is radial gauge Jacobian
Areal-gauge compensation condition:
