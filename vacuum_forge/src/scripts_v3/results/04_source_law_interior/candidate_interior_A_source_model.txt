============================================================================================================
Case 0: Areal-flux source law recap
============================================================================================================
Exact reduced source law:

  Delta_areal A = 8*pi*G*rho/c^2

where:

  Delta_areal A = (1/r^2)(r^2 A')'

Equivalent flux law:

  F_A(r) = 4*pi*r^2 A'
  F_A(r) = 8*pi*G*M_enc(r)/c^2

Interior test:

  M_enc(r) varies with r inside the source.
  Check whether A, A', and flux match smoothly to exterior.
[PASS] areal-flux law has interior enclosed-mass form

============================================================================================================
Case 1: Constant-density enclosed mass
============================================================================================================
rho(r) = rho0
M_enc(r) = 4*pi*r**3*rho0/3
M_enc'(r) = 4*pi*r**2*rho0
4*pi*r^2*rho0 = 4*pi*r**2*rho0
[PASS] M_enc derivative matches density volume element

============================================================================================================
Case 2: Interior A solution from flux law
============================================================================================================
M_enc(r) = 4*pi*r**3*rho0/3
A'(r) = 2G M_enc/(c^2 r^2) = 8*pi*G*r*rho0/(3*c**2)
A_in(r) = C + 4*pi*G*r**2*rho0/(3*c**2)

Delta_areal A_in = 8*pi*G*rho0/c**2
8*pi*G*rho0/c^2 = 8*pi*G*rho0/c**2
[PASS] interior A solves areal source equation

============================================================================================================
Case 3: Match interior to exterior at r=R
============================================================================================================
M = 4*pi*R**3*rho0/3
r_s = 8*pi*G*R**3*rho0/(3*c**2)
A_in = C + 4*pi*G*r**2*rho0/(3*c**2)
A_out = -8*pi*G*R**3*rho0/(3*c**2*r) + 1

A_in(R) = C + 4*pi*G*R**2*rho0/(3*c**2)
A_out(R) = -8*pi*G*R**2*rho0/(3*c**2) + 1
C solution = [-4*pi*G*R**2*rho0/c**2 + 1]

[PASS] A continuity fixes interior constant

A_in'(R) = 8*pi*G*R*rho0/(3*c**2)
A_out'(R) = 8*pi*G*R*rho0/(3*c**2)
[PASS] A' matches at boundary

============================================================================================================
Case 8: Not the full GR interior Schwarzschild solution
============================================================================================================
Important caution:

  The constant-density interior A(r) found here is the solution of
  the reduced areal-flux source law.

  It matches the weak-field Newtonian interior potential and matches
  smoothly to the exterior A=1-2GM/(rc²).

  But it is not the full GR interior Schwarzschild metric.

Reasons:
  - pressure is not included,
  - stress-energy is not fully represented,
  - the GR interior solution has a different exact lapse structure,
  - the current model enforces kappa=0 / B=1/A even inside.

[PASS] interior model is reduced-source toy, not full GR interior

============================================================================================================
Case 9: Summary classification
============================================================================================================
Results:

1. Constant density gives:
     M_enc(r) = (4π/3) rho0 r³

2. Areal flux law gives:
     A'(r) = 2G M_enc(r)/(c² r²)

3. Interior solution:
     A_in(r) = 1 - 4πG rho0 R²/c² + 4πG rho0 r²/(3c²)

4. Exterior solution:
     A_out(r) = 1 - 2GM/(c²r)

5. A and A' match at r=R.

6. Areal flux is continuous at r=R.

7. The origin is regular.

8. The weak-field Newtonian interior potential is recovered.

9. This is not the full GR interior Schwarzschild solution.

============================================================================================================
Final interpretation
============================================================================================================
The areal-flux law behaves sensibly for a finite constant-density
source in the reduced model.

It gives a regular interior A(r), smooth matching of A and A' at
the source boundary, continuous areal flux, and the correct exterior
Schwarzschild coefficient.

In weak field, the interior A(r) corresponds to the standard
Newtonian potential inside a uniform sphere.

However, this is not a full relativistic interior solution.
The next theoretical gap is pressure/stress and the question of whether
kappa=0 should hold inside matter or only in source-free exterior.
