Case 4: Local commutator check
========================================================================================================================
Let:

  O(x) = diag(p(x), q(x))
  v(x) = [a(x), b(x)]^T

Commutator:

  d(Ov)/dx - O dv/dx =
  Matrix([[a(x)*Derivative(p(x), x)], [b(x)*Derivative(q(x), x)]])

Expected leakage:

  (dO/dx)v =
  Matrix([[a(x)*Derivative(p(x), x)], [b(x)*Derivative(q(x), x)]])

If dO/dx = 0:

  commutator -> Matrix([[0], [0]])

Interpretation:
  A variable projector does not commute with derivatives.
  The leakage term is not optional; it must be assigned, canceled by theorem, or rejected.

Case 5: Projected divergence identity
========================================================================================================================
Formal requirement:

  nabla_mu(O X)^mu = O nabla_mu X^mu + (nabla_mu O) X^mu

Therefore projected conservation requires one of:

1. nabla_mu O = 0 on the relevant sector.
2. (nabla_mu O) X^mu is a derived neutral term.
3. boundary/source terms cancel by an explicit theorem.
4. O is diagnostic-only and does not alter the field equation.

It is not enough to say:

  O preserves Bianchi identity
  O preserves conservation
  O prevents overlap

without the commutator and boundary accounting.
