Case 4: Massless exterior consequence
========================================================================================================================
For massless kappa:

  kappa_ext ~ alpha_k Q_comp/(4*pi K_k r)

kappa_ext = Q_comp*alpha_k/(4*pi*K_k*r)

If Q_comp = 0, the monopole 1/r tail vanishes.

Higher multipoles or boundary-layer effects may remain, depending on source shape.

========================================================================================================================
Case 5: Parent identity requirement
========================================================================================================================
Compensation is mathematically useful but physically dangerous.

It is acceptable only if a parent identity demands something like:

  integral S_kappa d^3x = 0

or:

  kappa responds only to deviations from mean trace inside a constrained support

or:

  exterior kappa charge is projected out by a constraint equation

Otherwise the subtraction is just a hand-tuned fix.

========================================================================================================================
Case 6: Locality warning
========================================================================================================================
The subtraction:

  S_comp = S_trace - <S_trace>_support

is nonlocal over the support region.

That may be acceptable for a constraint projection, but not for a local
dynamical source law unless derived from a constrained variable.

Thus compensation points toward kappa as a constrained/non-propagating
trace response, not an ordinary local scalar field.
