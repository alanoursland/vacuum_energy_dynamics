Case 0: Kappa relaxation energy-accounting problem
========================================================================================================================
Question:

  If a kappa breathing/trace response exists, can it be damped or
  absorbed consistently rather than becoming long-range scalar radiation?

Rule:

  damping is not allowed as a cosmetic fix.

It must correspond to:

  energy transfer into a vacuum sink / restoring minimum
  or a constrained non-wave relaxation law

Preferred outcome:

  no ordinary breathing radiation

========================================================================================================================
Case 1: Undamped trace oscillator energy
========================================================================================================================
Toy undamped trace oscillator:

  kappa_dot = v
  v_dot = -omega0^2 kappa

Energy:

E = kappa**2*omega_0**2/2 + v**2/2

This energy is conserved in the toy oscillator.
If coupled to exterior wave modes, it can support breathing radiation.

========================================================================================================================
Case 2: Damped oscillator energy derivative
========================================================================================================================
Damped trace oscillator:

  kappa_dot = v
  v_dot = -Gamma v - omega0^2 kappa

Energy:

E = kappa**2*omega_0**2/2 + v**2/2

Energy derivative:

dE/dt = -Gamma*v**2

For Gamma > 0:

  dE/dt <= 0

The lost energy must be accounted for as vacuum absorption/dissipation.

========================================================================================================================
Case 3: Critical damping condition
========================================================================================================================
For toy equation:

  kappa_ddot + Gamma kappa_dot + omega0^2 kappa = 0

Critical damping condition:

Eq(Gamma**2, 4*omega_0**2)

Regimes:

  Gamma^2 < 4 omega0^2  -> underdamped oscillatory trace response
  Gamma^2 = 4 omega0^2  -> critically damped
  Gamma^2 > 4 omega0^2  -> overdamped

Cleanest fallback if breathing is forced:

  critical/overdamped, not underdamped propagating.

========================================================================================================================
Case 4: Relaxation sink balance
========================================================================================================================
Damping power lost from kappa oscillator:

  P_loss = Gamma kappa_dot^2

P_loss = Gamma*v**2

Vacuum balance must include a sink term:

  Gamma_relax = P_loss

or an equivalent positive-definite absorption term.

Without this, damping is just a hand-wave.

========================================================================================================================
Case 5: Static A-constraint guard
========================================================================================================================
Relaxation must not erase static scalar gravity.

Allowed:

  damp kappa trace/breathing deviations
  absorb scalar radiative trace perturbations

Not allowed:

  damp the static A_constraint mass field
  remove areal flux
  alter A = 1 - 2GM/(c^2 r)

Therefore relaxation must act on:

  kappa_rad / trace deviation

not on:

  A_constraint

Case 7: Attenuation length criterion
========================================================================================================================
For a disturbance moving at characteristic speed c with damping Gamma:

  attenuation length L_att ~ c/Gamma

L_att = c/Gamma

To avoid long-range breathing radiation:

  L_att must be short compared with observational propagation scales

or the mode must be non-propagating/constrained.

This gives a future phenomenological bound if damping is used.
