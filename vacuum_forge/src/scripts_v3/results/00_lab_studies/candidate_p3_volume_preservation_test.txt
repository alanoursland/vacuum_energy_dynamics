======================================================================
Move 1 experiment: P3 → volume preservation → trace-kernel?
======================================================================

Result: The question reformulates rather than resolves.

P1 (vacuum is energy) + P3 (rho_v constant) together imply that
vacuum volume and vacuum energy are the same quantity, related by
the constant rho_v. So 'exchange that preserves volume' and
'exchange that preserves local energy' are the same thing.

The trace-kernel constraint follows IF exchange is defined as
'no net local energy change.' But this definition is not forced
by P1-P5 as currently stated. It would require a strengthened
interpretation of P5 (or a new postulate) saying that local
matter-vacuum interaction in static configurations cannot inject
or extract net energy at the matter's location.

So Move 1 does NOT close the equal-response gap from P3 alone.
It identifies that the gap is in the definition of 'exchange'
vs 'creation,' which P1-P5 currently does not pin down.

Suggested next move: examine whether 'exchange = no local net
energy change' follows from P5 under any natural strengthening,
or whether it must be adopted as a separate postulate.
