# 10. Discussion

The main lesson is that the projection hierarchy is a boundary-adapted moment
construction.  The rational coefficient is forced by a beta moment ratio, and
the higher coefficients are generated by increasing endpoint contact order.

The second lesson is methodological.  Determinant searches are useful for
discovering structure, but the determinant sequence does not have to be
positive to prove finite-system invertibility.  Once the matrix is recognized
as a cross-Gram matrix, the relevant object is the nondegenerate bilinear
pairing between two bases of an admissible subspace.

This also clarifies the role of the original `R = 0` coefficient.  It is not a
special numerical coincidence.  It is the base endpoint-admissibility level of
a larger ladder:

```text
R = 0,1,2,...
```

with

```text
r_{R,k} = (2k - 1)/(2k + 2R + 3).
```

For future use, this paper should be read as a mathematical module.  A
separate physical argument may select a particular contact level, weight, or
domain, but those choices are external to the algebra proved here.
