# 8. Relation to Existing f(R) Literature

This section is a placeholder until the novelty pass is complete.

Known background that must be addressed:

- `f(R)` gravity is dynamically equivalent to a scalar-tensor theory under
  standard transformations.
- `R + a R^2` contains a massive scalaron with `m^2 = 1/(6a)`.
- Static `f(R)` star exteriors generally differ from Schwarzschild and can
  have `g_tt g_rr != -1`.
- Screening and thin-shell mechanisms can suppress scalar signatures in more
  general `f(R)` models.
- Existing no-hair theorems may cover overlapping assumptions.

The candidate novelty is narrower:

- formulate `AB = 1` as a static frame-indifference selection principle;
- show that scalaron hair is mandatory in a compact matching witness;
- combine mandatory hair with the anisotropy lemma to force `a = 0`;
- treat short-range experiments as tests of the principle rather than fitted
  bounds on `a`.

The final paper should not claim that `f(R)` hair itself is new. It should
claim only what survives the literature check.
