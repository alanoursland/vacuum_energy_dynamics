# Abstract

We show that a static frame-indifference principle selects the
Einstein-Hilbert action from the healthy local `R + a R^2` extension. In
static spherical areal gauge, the principle is represented by

```text
g_tt g_rr = -1
```

or equivalently `A(r) B(r) = 1`: a strictly static source-free exterior should
carry no intrinsic preferred temporal-radial frame. In `R + a R^2` gravity,
the higher-derivative scalar response carries temporal-radial anisotropy
whenever the scalar curvature profile is nontrivial. In the conventions used
here, the relevant response difference is proportional to

```text
D^t_t - D^r_r = -R'' .
```

We then show that scalaron hair is mandatory for sourced static bodies when
`a != 0`. Fourth-order matching of `R` and `R'` at the surface admits no
hairless exterior; in the uniform-density witness, the would-be hairless
condition reduces to `x cosh x = sinh x`, which has no positive solution.
Thus every nonzero `a` produces static exterior scalaron hair, and every such
hair violates static frame indifference. The selection principle therefore
forces `a = 0`, leaving Einstein gravity with a cosmological term and inert
Gauss-Bonnet freedom. If the principle is dropped, the scalaron gives a
gravitational-strength Yukawa channel with fixed coupling `alpha = 1/3`;
short-range experiments then bound its range rather than eliminating it.
