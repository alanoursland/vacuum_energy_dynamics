# 11. Conclusion

The stable gravitational sector is not positive definite. It is
sector-indefinite.

The static self-coupling bootstrap forces the scalar/static energy density
to have the negative sign

$$
u_{\rm stat}=-\frac{c^4}{8\pi G}(s')^2 .
$$

If that sector were allowed to propagate, its Hamiltonian would be
unbounded below. Stability therefore forces it to be an elliptic
constraint. The source-free constraint equation then removes the remaining
mining worry: flat vacuum is the unique regular asymptotically flat
zero-source state, so the negative static sector is a functional of
sources rather than an independent reservoir. The sector that does
propagate, the transverse-traceless radiative sector, has positive
sum-of-squares energy and outward null flux.

The final normalization of the radiative sector is not available at linear
order. Linear work-flux balance is underdetermined in `K_T`. The
coefficient is fixed only when the wave's own energy is included as a
source at the same universal coupling as all other energy.

This gives a compact answer to the conformal/static-sector puzzle:
gravity survives its wrong-sign sector because that sector is not a
radiative degree of freedom. The negative mode is constrained and
source-bound; the propagating modes are positive.

The broader Vacuum Energy Dynamics program uses this sector architecture
as one component in a derivation of the Einstein field equations. The
present note makes the narrower point independently: a stable
self-coupled gravitational field has to assign the signs and dynamical
characters in exactly this way.
