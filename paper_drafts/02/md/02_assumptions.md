# 2. Minimal Assumptions and Notation

The purpose of this paper is not to derive the full field equations. It is
to isolate the sign-and-stability architecture of a self-coupled
gravitational field. We therefore use a deliberately small set of
assumptions.

First, in the static spherical exterior we write

$$
ds^2=-c^2A(r)\,dt^2+B(r)\,dr^2+r^2d\Omega^2 .
$$

The areal radius $r$ is fixed by the area of the symmetry spheres. The
remaining temporal-radial information is represented by $A(r)$ and
$B(r)$.

Second, define

$$
s(r)=\frac{1}{2}\ln\frac{A(r)}{B(r)} ,
\qquad
\kappa(r)=\frac{1}{2}\ln(A(r)B(r)).
$$

The variable $s$ is the static shear sector. In the compensated static
exterior, $\kappa=0$, so $A B=1$ and $A=e^s$.

Third, the static response obeys an areal flux law in the self-coupled
variable:

$$
\Delta_{\rm ar}A=\frac{8\pi G}{c^2}\rho,
\qquad
\Delta_{\rm ar}f=\frac{1}{r^2}(r^2 f')'.
$$

The normalization is fixed by the Newtonian limit. No post-Newtonian or
radiative observations are used to set coefficients in this paper.

Fourth, field energy is counted as a gravitational source once it appears
in the adopted response law. This is the self-coupling condition. It does
not allow arbitrary named reservoirs to gravitate; it requires energy
already present in the dynamics to be counted with the same universal
coupling as other energy.

Fifth, stable vacuum dynamics may not contain a propagating degree of
freedom whose Hamiltonian is unbounded below.

Finally, the radiative sector is the transverse-traceless sector of a
massless spin-2 perturbation. We use the conventional symbol
$h_{ij}^{\rm TT}$ for this sector and denote its kinetic normalization by
$K_T$.

These assumptions are enough to derive the result of interest: the static
sector has negative energy, the negative sector must be constrained, the
source-free static sector is not mineable, and the propagating sector has
positive energy.
