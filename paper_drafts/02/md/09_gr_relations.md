# 9. Relation to Standard GR Treatments

The result should not be oversold as a new discovery that GR has a
constraint structure. That is standard. In Hamiltonian formulations of
general relativity, the conformal danger is controlled by constraints, and
the physical radiative degrees of freedom are the transverse-traceless
modes.

The contribution of this note is a different route to the same
architecture. Rather than beginning with the full ADM formulation, the
argument begins with static self-coupling and stability:

$$
\text{self-coupling}\rightarrow u_{\rm stat}<0,
$$

then

$$
u_{\rm stat}<0+\text{stability}
\rightarrow
\text{constraint, not radiation}.
$$

The source-free uniqueness theorem then removes the mineability worry,
and the TT calculation identifies the positive radiative sector.

This route is close in spirit to spin-2 bootstrap arguments, but the focus
is narrower. Spin-2 bootstrap derivations ask how a massless tensor field
coupled to its own stress-energy closes into nonlinear gravity. The
present note asks why the sign structure of that closure is stable: which
sector may propagate, which sector must remain constrained, and why linear
radiation theory cannot determine its own normalization.

The relation to the conformal-factor problem is also direct. The standard
problem is that the conformal direction in the gravitational action has
the wrong sign. The resolution is not to turn that mode into a healthy
radiative particle. The resolution is that it is not a radiative particle.
It belongs to the constraint sector. The present derivation gives a
short bootstrap route to that assignment.
