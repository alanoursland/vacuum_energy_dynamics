# Appendix D. Four-Derivative Gate

The local four-derivative action candidates reduce to three classes:

- Weyl/spin-2 curvature-squared terms;
- the Gauss-Bonnet invariant;
- `R^2`.

The first class introduces a massive spin-2 ghost and is excluded by
stability. Gauss-Bonnet is topological in four dimensions and does not change
the local response. The remaining `R^2` term is healthy at the linear level for
`a > 0`, with scalaron range

```text
ell* = sqrt(6a) .
```

The scalaron is eliminated by P7-prime. Any nonzero `a` gives mandatory static
hair around sources. That hair violates `AB = 1` in the static source-free
exterior. Thus:

```text
a = 0 .
```
