# 11. Open Obligations

The obligations split into two categories: rigor debts on the closed
gravitational result and open physics outside that result.

Rigor debts:

- replace the cited self-coupled spin-2 uniqueness theorem with an in-house
  Deser-style closure proof;
- lift the reduced static, radiative, vector, and cosmological patch proofs to
  fully covariant statements;
- formalize Isaacson averaging, secular-term control, and gauge invariance of
  the radiative stress tensor;
- prove nonlinear stability beyond the reduced sector;
- complete the tensor-virial identity in generality;
- rewrite older P8-language theorem statements under the modern P9 bootstrap.

Open physics:

- derive or constrain the value of `Lambda`;
- give dynamics to any dark-sector frustration excess;
- determine whether the substance frame has any non-gravitational observable
  channel;
- close the interior cap/strong-compactness program;
- sharpen the Casimir/UFFT sector and its falsifiers;
- build a public archive with scripts, provenance, and DOI.

None of these obligations moves the coefficients stated in Section 8. They
determine whether the theory becomes a polished, self-contained framework and
whether it produces non-GR numbers outside the closed gravitational sector.
