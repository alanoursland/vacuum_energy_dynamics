# 7. Eliminating Four-Derivative Freedom

The two-derivative closure permits the Einstein-Hilbert response, but local
four-derivative curvature terms must still be addressed. The ghost gate
removes most of them.

Quadratic curvature terms that modify the transverse-traceless propagator
introduce a massive spin-2 pole with negative residue. Since the TT sector is
propagating, that pole is a ghost and is excluded by the same stability logic
that caged the static scalar sector.

In four spacetime dimensions, Gauss-Bonnet is topological and inert for the
local field equations. The only healthy local four-derivative survivor is

```text
a R^2 .
```

For `a > 0`, this term carries a massive scalar mode, often called the
scalaron, with range

```text
ell* = sqrt(6a) .
```

The scalaron is not a propagating TT ghost. It is therefore not killed by the
first ghost gate.

P7-prime kills it. A nonzero scalaron range produces mandatory static exterior
hair around sources. That hair creates a preferred t-r structure in static
vacuum and violates the P7-prime shadow condition `AB = 1`. The no-hair escape
is unavailable because fourth-order matching requires nonzero scalaron exterior
profile for any ordinary source when `a != 0`.

Therefore

```text
a = 0 .
```

This is a strong negative result. The theory does not predict a short-range
gravitational Yukawa correction. Instead, any gravitational-strength Yukawa
detection would attack P7-prime and reopen the four-derivative sector.
