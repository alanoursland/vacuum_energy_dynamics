# 4. Static Bookkeeping and the Exact Exterior

The first nontrivial closure occurs in the static spherical sector. Use
areal-radius coordinates

```text
ds^2 = -A(r)c^2 dt^2 + B(r) dr^2 + r^2 dOmega^2 .
```

The source law has Gauss form. Curvature is the spatial differential of vacuum
amount, and a localized source conditions the surrounding vacuum
isotropically. With the Newton normalization, the areal-flux law is

```text
Delta_areal A = (8 pi G / c^2) rho .
```

Equivalently,

```text
4 pi r^2 A'(r) = (8 pi G / c^2) M_enc(r) .
```

P7-prime supplies the static exterior compensation condition. In a strictly
static source-free exterior, the vacuum carries no energy current and no
preferred t-r frame. Its metric shadow is

```text
A(r) B(r) = 1 .
```

P9 then fixes the nonlinear variable and the sign of the configuration energy.
Write

```text
s = (1/2) ln(A/B) .
```

The exponential identity

```text
Delta_areal(e^s) = e^s [Delta_areal s + (s')^2]
```

shows that the flux law is linear in `A = e^s` precisely when the shear sector
contains

```text
Delta_areal s = -(s')^2 + source .
```

Thus the static field-energy density is

```text
u_field = -c^4 (s')^2 / (8 pi G) .
```

The sign is forced by the self-coupling identity and P9's count-once
placement. It is not chosen to match GR.

In exterior vacuum, the areal-flux equation gives

```text
A(r) = 1 - 2GM/(c^2 r),
B(r) = A(r)^(-1).
```

The Schwarzschild exterior appears here as a result of bookkeeping. No tensor
field equation has yet been used.
