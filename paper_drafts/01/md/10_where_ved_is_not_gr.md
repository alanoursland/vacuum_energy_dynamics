# 10. Where VED Is Not GR

If the field equations are Einstein's, one might ask whether the theory is
only GR in different words. The answer is no, but the difference is not a
local gravitational deviation in the closed sector.

First, the metric is interpreted as vacuum substance configuration. In GR,
local gravitational field energy is not uniquely defined. In VED, the static
sector carries a definite local energy density:

```text
u_field = -c^4 (s')^2 / (8 pi G) .
```

This density is not decorative. It is part of the P9 ledger that fixes the
static bootstrap.

Second, the vacuum has a substance frame. The closed gravitational equations
quarantine couplings to that frame, so no current gravitational experiment
should see a preferred-frame deviation. But the ontology says the frame exists,
and future vacuum-sector physics must respect the quarantine rules.

Third, `Lambda` has a proposed owner. In GR, it can simply be a constant. In
VED, it is expected to arise from a vacuum frustration floor. This paper does
not derive its value.

Fourth, the dark sector is not inserted as a modification of the gravitational
field equations. The live route is a vacuum-sector excess over the frustration
floor, if such a sector can be given dynamics without violating the closed
gravitational response.

Finally, the theory retains non-gravitational vacuum-sector seams, including
Casimir/UFFT-style questions and strong-interior admissibility. These are not
licensed as arbitrary add-ons. They are marked doors for later work, each
requiring its own gate discipline.
