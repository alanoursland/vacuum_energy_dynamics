# 2. Inputs and Anti-Circularity Rules

The framework imports special relativity but not general relativity. The
imported content includes local Lorentz invariance, Minkowski structure in the
zero-source state, the invariant speed `c`, mass-energy equivalence, and local
validity of special relativity. These imports provide the kinematic background
for matter and fields before gravity is derived.

The live postulate set is:

| label | content | role |
|---|---|---|
| P1 | vacuum is energy | makes vacuum bookkeeping physical |
| P2 | vacuum configuration is spacetime | supplies metric/matter-response ontology |
| P3 | vacuum has constant local energy density | supports compensated configuration language |
| P3a | curvature is spatial differential of vacuum amount | identifies gradients as curvature content |
| P4 | curvature configurations contain energy | gives field configurations a ledger entry |
| P5 | configurations settle toward minimum energy | supplies stability selection |
| P6 | energy moving in gradients exchanges with vacuum | gives redshift and source-law bookkeeping |
| P7-prime | strictly static vacuum has no energy current or preferred t-r frame | yields `AB = 1` in static exteriors |
| P9 | configuration energy gravitates universally, counted once | fixes self-coupling and response normalization |

Two inputs require special emphasis. P7-prime and P9 are fenced
commitments. They are not hidden consequences of P1-P6, and they are not
presented as empirical fits. P7-prime is a static frame-indifference rule for
source-free vacuum configurations. P9 is a source-ledger rule: configuration
energy gravitates at the universal coupling and must not be counted twice.

The anti-circularity rules are:

- do not assume the Einstein equations;
- do not assume Schwarzschild as a solution;
- do not tune post-Newtonian coefficients except for the single Newton
  normalization `G`;
- do not use observational tests as fitted targets after Newton's `G`;
- do not hide P7-prime or P9 as if they followed from P1-P6;
- separate reduced-sector theorems from conditional covariant closure.

The retired commitments P7 and P8 remain in the archive only as historical
scaffolding. P7 is now the metric shadow of P7-prime. P8's multiplicative
temporal self-coupling is now a theorem under P9 and the static bootstrap.
