# 1. Introduction

General relativity usually begins with geometry. Matter tells spacetime how to
curve, spacetime tells matter how to move, and the Einstein field equations
state the local response. This paper asks a different question: can the same
field equations be obtained from a non-geometric ontology in which the vacuum
is a physical substance, energy and spacetime are the same underlying content,
and gravitational response is fixed by energy bookkeeping?

The target is not a phenomenological fit to known post-Newtonian coefficients.
The program is instead to start from stated inputs, derive sector constraints,
and allow each possible deviation from general relativity to face a kill
condition. In the current version, the gravitational sector closes not on a
new modified-gravity equation but on the Einstein equation with a cosmological
term.

This outcome should be read carefully. The claim is not that general
relativity is wrong in its tested gravitational sector. The claim is that the
same local gravitational response can be reached from a different ontology:
vacuum substance rather than primitive geometry. The resulting theory agrees
with GR in the closed gravitational sector while differing in its account of
what the metric is, what static field energy is, what the cosmological term
means, and which vacuum-sector questions remain open.

The derivation proceeds in layers. First, the postulates and anti-circularity
rules define the allowed inputs. Second, the vacuum configuration is argued to
carry a symmetric tensor metric variable with relabeling gauge freedom and
universal matter response. Third, static bookkeeping derives the exact
Schwarzschild exterior and the negative static field-energy density without
using the Einstein equations. Fourth, stability forces the sign-indefinite
sector architecture: the negative static sector is source-bound and
non-propagating, while transverse-traceless radiation is positive. Fifth,
spin-2 closure and radiative self-coupling fix the dynamical normalization.
Finally, the four-derivative sector is eliminated, leaving the Einstein-Hilbert
response plus a permitted cosmological term.

The paper is written for auditability. Each claim is either part of the
postulate set, a reduced-sector theorem, a standard field-theory closure result
used with stated scope, or an open obligation. The strongest technical proofs
are delegated to supporting papers and appendices; this paper gives the
coherent derivation map.
