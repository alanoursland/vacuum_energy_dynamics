# 5. Sector Architecture and Stability

The static bookkeeping result contains a negative local field-energy density.
That fact would be fatal if the negative sector propagated as an ordinary
wave. The stability analysis therefore matters as much as the exterior
solution.

The sector architecture is:

| sector | energy sign | character |
|---|---|---|
| static scalar/shear | negative | elliptic, source-slaved |
| transverse-traceless radiation | positive | hyperbolic, propagating |
| vector sector | bounded magnetic-type constraint | sourced by momentum |
| trace/kappa | suppressed in static vacuum | cosmological correction only |

The negative sector cannot be promoted to a propagating mode. If it were
hyperbolic, its Hamiltonian would be unbounded below:

```text
H[lambda s] = lambda^2 H[s] -> -infinity .
```

P5's minimum-energy stability requirement therefore forces the static sector
to be a constraint, not a radiative degree of freedom.

The negative sector also cannot be mined in source-free vacuum. The static
constraint equation with flatness and regularity has the unique zero-source
solution:

```text
A = 1 .
```

The static field is a functional of sources, not an independent reservoir.
Changing it requires moving sources, and the associated energy accounting runs
through the positive radiative channel.

The propagating transverse-traceless sector has positive energy and outward
null flux. This is the same stability architecture that lets GR survive its
wrong-sign conformal direction: the sign-indefinite sector is caged by
constraints, while the radiative sector carries positive energy.

Paper 2 gives the detailed proof core for this architecture. Here it functions
as the bridge from static bookkeeping to dynamical closure.
