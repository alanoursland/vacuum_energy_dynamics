# 3. Case Study: Vacuum-Substance Gravity

The case study is a vacuum-substance gravity program. The theory begins from
the idea that vacuum is physical substance, energy and spacetime are identified
at the ontological level, and gravitational phenomena should be derived through
vacuum-configuration bookkeeping.

The starting goal was not to recover GR by stipulation. The program explored
candidate mechanisms for vacuum-energy dynamics, dark-sector behavior,
short-range modifications, boundary smoothing, and field-equation closure. The
archive currently contains thirteen field-equation trial groups under
`vacuum_forge/src/field_equation_trials`, including a trial charter, postulate
adoption records, static bootstrap work, radiative positivity gates,
radiative bootstrap scripts, boundary-admissibility tests, higher-curvature
health gates, cosmology checks, and vector-sector closure.

The gravitational-sector result was conservative: the surviving response was

```text
G_ab + Lambda g_ab = (8 pi G / c^4) T_ab .
```

The program did not preserve the owner's preferred deviations. Candidate
routes such as tidal vacuum nucleation, depletion-history dark matter,
burden-reduction long-range forces, boundary smoothing, scalaron hair, and
accessible gravitational Yukawa corrections were killed or fenced.

The case study is useful because the methodology can be evaluated without
accepting the final physics. The process produced artifacts: scripts, gates,
adoption records, kill ledgers, downgrade banners, and provenance notes. These
artifacts make the AI-assisted work auditable in a way that ordinary fluent
conversation with a model would not be.
