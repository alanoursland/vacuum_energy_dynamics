# 9. Failure Case: Artifact Versus Intuition

The workflow is not self-executing. It can fail. One recorded failure mode is
the artifact-versus-intuition problem.

In the TVN incident, an AI assistant formalized a mechanism and then killed
the formalized version. The kill may have been mathematically valid for the
artifact, but the artifact did not faithfully capture the theory owner's
actual intuition. The process had moved too quickly from intuition to formal
candidate.

The failure was not that a candidate was killed. Killing candidates is the
point. The failure was that the target was not adequately confirmed before the
gate was built.

The corrective protocol is:

- separate intuition capture from formal candidate construction;
- label early formalizations as probes, not adopted candidates;
- require owner confirmation before a probe becomes gate-eligible;
- record when a killed artifact is not a killed owner commitment;
- preserve the incident as a methodology warning.

This section is important because it prevents overclaiming. Adversarial AI
workflows can produce false confidence if the AI attacks a caricature of the
human idea. The archive must distinguish "this formal candidate failed" from
"the original intuition was refuted."
