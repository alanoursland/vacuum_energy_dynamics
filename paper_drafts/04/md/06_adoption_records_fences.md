# 6. Workflow Component 3: Adoption Records and Fences

Not every theory move is derived. Some are postulates, conventions, or
theory-owner choices. The workflow requires these moves to be explicit.

An adoption record should state:

- what is being adopted;
- who owns the adoption;
- when it was adopted;
- why it is being adopted;
- what it does not license;
- which future gates can test or appeal it.

The norm is:

```text
Do not call a postulate a theorem.
Do not hide an adoption inside a proof.
Do not let a successful downstream consequence retroactively make the adoption look derived.
```

In the case study, P9 and P7-prime are the main examples. P9 states that
configuration energy gravitates at the universal coupling, counted once.
P7-prime states a static frame-indifference condition for strictly static
vacuum. Both were human-owned commitments with fences. Their strongest
consequences, including the static bootstrap closure and the elimination of
the four-derivative scalar, were discovered downstream.

The fence matters. A fenced adoption can be used inside its declared scope and
tested by its declared consequences. It cannot be smuggled into unrelated
sectors as a universal license.
