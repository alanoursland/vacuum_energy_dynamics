# 13. Conclusion

AI-assisted theoretical work is risky because language models are fluent. They
can make weak arguments sound coherent and can help a researcher protect a
preferred theory. The solution is not to pretend the model is reliable. The
solution is to place it inside an adversarial workflow.

The workflow described here uses charters, kill conditions, executable
derivation checks, dependency ledgers, adoption fences, downgrade records, and
data provenance grades. In the vacuum-substance gravity case study, this
discipline killed multiple attractive mechanisms and forced the gravitational
sector to close on GR rather than on a desired deviation.

The broader lesson is that AI systems can be useful in theory development when
they are not treated as authorities. They are useful as accelerators of
candidate generation, derivation, criticism, and executable checking. The
trustworthy object is not the model's prose. It is the audited workflow around
the prose.
