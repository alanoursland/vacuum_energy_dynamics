# Abstract

Large language models are increasingly used in theoretical research, but their
fluency makes methodology more important, not less. We describe an
adversarial workflow for AI-assisted theoretical physics and report a completed
case study from a vacuum-substance gravity program. The workflow requires
predeclared charters with kill conditions, machine-executable derivation
scripts, explicit dependency ledgers, timestamped human adoption records for
new postulates, honest downgrade and supersession records, and provenance
grades for data used in empirical confrontations. In the case study, the
workflow killed multiple mechanisms proposed or entertained by the theory
owner and closed the gravitational sector on general relativity rather than on
the owner's preferred deviations. We analyze the workflow components, the
artifacts they produce, and their limits. We also discuss a failure case in
which an AI assistant formalized and killed a mechanism before the owner's
actual intuition had been adequately captured. The result is a practical
methodology for using AI systems as adversarial derivation assistants rather
than persuasive coauthors.
