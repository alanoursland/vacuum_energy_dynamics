# 12. Recommendations

For researchers using AI systems in theoretical work:

- write a charter before the derivation begins;
- define verdict states before seeing the result;
- require at least one explicit kill condition for every candidate;
- separate postulates, conventions, theorems, probes, and guesses;
- keep human-owned adoption records for new assumptions;
- write executable scripts for algebraic and numerical claims when possible;
- declare dependencies on upstream results;
- preserve killed candidates and downgrade records;
- attach provenance grades to empirical numbers;
- distinguish AI-generated formal artifacts from confirmed owner intuitions;
- disclose AI assistance according to venue rules;
- publish enough archive structure for readers to audit the process.

The central practical norm is adversarial placement. Do not ask an AI system
to make a theory look good. Ask it to generate candidates, formalize them,
attack them, and help build scripts that make the attack reproducible.

The human role is not removed. It becomes more explicit: choose the questions,
own the assumptions, approve formalizations, judge scope, and decide when a
result is ready for public claim.
