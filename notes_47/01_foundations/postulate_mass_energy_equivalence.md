# Postulate 3: Mass-Energy Equivalence

---

## Statement

**Postulate 3 (Mass-Energy Equivalence).** All forms of energy participate in vacuum exchanges equivalently per unit energy. A photon of energy $E$ behaves, for the purposes of vacuum exchange, as if it possessed mass $E/c^2$, even though its rest mass is zero. Rest mass-energy, kinetic energy, electromagnetic energy, binding energy, field energy, and any other form of energy all count equally.

This postulate is not an independent assumption. It follows from special relativity, which the framework adopts as background. We state it explicitly because it does heavy lifting in subsequent derivations, and having it named as a postulate simplifies reference.

---

## What the Postulate Does

Postulate 2 commits to the gravitational force acting uniformly on every quantum of energy, with equal force per unit energy regardless of the kind. This postulate specifies what counts as a quantum of energy for that purpose: every form of energy identified by special relativity as mass-equivalent, including rest mass-energy, kinetic energy, and all other forms. Photons with zero rest mass are full participants through their total energy $E$, which enters vacuum exchanges as $E/c^2$ of effective mass.

Together, Postulate 2 and Postulate 3 give the framework the equivalence principle: gravity acts on all energy equally, and all forms of energy count. This is what makes the framework's treatment of mass and light uniform, and what allows derivations of gravitational effects on photons (such as gravitational redshift) to proceed from the same foundation as derivations about massive particles.

---

## Status

The equivalence of mass and energy was established in 1905 by Einstein as a consequence of special relativity. The relation $E = mc^2$ is a theorem of SR, not an additional postulate of SR. Since our framework adopts SR in full, mass-energy equivalence comes with it automatically.

We state this as a postulate rather than deriving it freshly in each application because it is used repeatedly. Derivations of gravitational redshift, of how photons respond to curvature gradients, and of any other phenomenon involving energy exchanges across different kinds of energy will invoke this postulate. Having it explicit in its own document makes those invocations cleaner.
