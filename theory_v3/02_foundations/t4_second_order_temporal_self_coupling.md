## Setup

Consider a static, source-free exterior vacuum configuration around a
localized mass constraint. The assumptions are:

- staticity;
- an exterior region outside the matter source;
- source-free vacuum in that exterior region;
- weak field, $U/c^2 \ll 1$;
- asymptotic flatness;
- the P9+C2 temporal-composition theorem above.

The temporal scale factor is

$$\alpha(r)=\sqrt{-g_{tt}(r)}.$$

## Derivation

By the P9+C2 temporal-composition theorem,

$$d\ln\alpha=-\frac{dU}{c^2}+O(c^{-6}).$$

Integrating from spatial infinity to radius $r$ gives

$$\ln\alpha(r)-\ln\alpha(\infty)
=-\frac{U(r)-U(\infty)}{c^2}+O(c^{-6}).$$

Asymptotic flatness gives

$$\alpha(\infty)=1,$$

and

$$U(\infty)=0.$$

Therefore

$$\ln\alpha(r)=-\frac{U(r)}{c^2}+O(c^{-6}).$$

Exponentiating through second order,

$$\alpha(r)
=1-\frac{U}{c^2}+\frac{1}{2}\frac{U^2}{c^4}+O(c^{-6}).$$

Since

$$-g_{tt}=\alpha^2,$$

we get

$$
-g_{tt}
=\left(1-\frac{U}{c^2}
+\frac{1}{2}\frac{U^2}{c^4}
+O(c^{-6})\right)^2.
$$

Keeping terms through $O(c^{-4})$,

$$-g_{tt}=1-\frac{2U}{c^2}+2\frac{U^2}{c^4}+O(c^{-6}).$$

Thus

$$\beta=1.$$
