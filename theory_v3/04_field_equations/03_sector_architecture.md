## The three load-bearing theorems

**1. The negative sector cannot propagate (ghost exclusion, G03/T2).**
Promoting the static sector to a hyperbolic mode gives a Hamiltonian
unbounded below ($H[\lambda s]=\lambda^2 H[s]\to-\infty$) draining into
the positive TT sector. Stability therefore *forces* the
elliptic/constraint assignment. What the old FEC-007 program adopted as
policy ("A is constraint, not radiation") is a theorem.

**2. The negative sector cannot be mined (source-binding, G02).**
$(r^2A')'=0$ with flatness and regularity has the unique solution
$A\equiv1$: the static sector is a functional of its sources, not an
independent reservoir. Extracting its energy requires moving sources,
and the proceeds radiate through the positive channel — energy
accounting closes.

**3. Everything that propagates is positive (G03 + 008).** The TT energy
density is a sum of squares with derived coefficient $c^2/32\pi G$ per
$\langle\dot h_{ij}\dot h_{ij}\rangle$; flux = c × density (null
transport); the binary-pulsar spin-down anchors the sign and the
derived magnitude reproduces it exactly.

## The boundary corollary

source, curvature smoothness at matter boundaries equals source

energy is finite for arbitrarily sharp boundaries (it reads $(s')^2$,

approaches $r_s$ — locating the theory's genuine strong-field frontier
at the interior cap, not at edges.
