# P4: Curvature Contains Energy

## What This Postulate Says

Curvature contains energy. The configuration of the vacuum carries energy in addition to the substance energy of the vacuum present. A region of curved vacuum has more total energy than a region of flat vacuum containing the same amount of vacuum substance, with the difference being the configuration energy of the curved arrangement.

The postulate makes three commitments stacked together.

The vacuum has a configuration energy, distinct from its substance energy. The substance energy of a region is determined by P3's constant density times the amount of vacuum present (P3a's spatial differential structure determines what "amount" means in a region of given geometric extent). Configuration energy is a separate contribution that depends on how the vacuum is arranged — on the curvature pattern across neighboring regions.

The configuration energy depends on directional structure. The amount of vacuum per unit geometric extent varies with direction at each point, as we noted in P3a. Configurations with different directional patterns of vacuum amount have different configuration energies. Configuration energy is a functional of this directional structure, not just of any single scalar quantity.

The total energy of vacuum in a region is substance energy plus configuration energy. Both are real, both contribute to the region's total energy content, and both must be tracked in any complete energy accounting.

---

## The Two Energies of Vacuum

The framework now has two distinct energy types associated with the vacuum, and they do different work.

**Substance energy** is the energy of vacuum-as-stuff. It is determined by P3's constant density times the amount of vacuum present. It is intensive in the per-unit-vacuum sense (each unit of vacuum carries the same energy by P3) but extensive in the total-amount sense (regions with more vacuum have more substance energy). Substance energy doesn't depend on how the vacuum is arranged, only on how much is there.

**Configuration energy** is the energy of vacuum-as-arrangement. It depends on the curvature pattern — on how vacuum amount varies across neighboring regions and in different directions. Two regions with the same total amount of vacuum can have different configuration energies if their curvature patterns differ. Configuration energy can be present without any change in substance amount (a region whose vacuum is rearranged but not added to or removed from has different configuration energy but the same substance energy).

These are independent properties of the same substance. Changing configuration without changing substance amount changes configuration energy but not substance energy. Changing substance amount typically changes both, since the new vacuum changes both the total amount present and the configuration the vacuum is in.

The two-energy structure is what makes the framework's later content possible. Local gravitational dynamics will turn out to involve configuration changes without substance changes (configuration regime). Other physical processes will involve real substance creation or destruction (substance regime). Both regimes use configuration energy and substance energy, but in different roles. P4 establishes that both energies exist; later postulates and consequences develop how they interact.

---

## The Directional Structure of Configuration Energy

P3a noted that vacuum amount per unit geometric extent is directional at each point. The amount of vacuum corresponding to a small displacement depends on which direction the displacement runs along. This directional dependence is part of the framework's curvature content.

Configuration energy depends on this directional structure. The energy of a configuration is a functional of the directional pattern of vacuum amount, not just of a single scalar describing total vacuum content per region.

Several features of this dependence are worth flagging.

**Configuration energy is local.** It is determined by the directional pattern of vacuum amount in a region and its neighbors. Specifying the pattern locally specifies the local configuration energy.

**Configuration energy is relational.** Like curvature in P3a, configuration energy lives in the relationships between regions, not within any region considered alone. A single region's contribution to configuration energy depends on how that region's vacuum amount relates to its neighbors' amounts in different directions.

**Configuration energy is direction-sensitive.** A configuration where vacuum amount varies in one direction differently than in another has different configuration energy than a configuration with isotropic variation. The functional that gives configuration energy from the directional pattern can distinguish these cases.

**Configuration energy has tensor-like content.** A scalar field can encode "how much vacuum is here" but not "how vacuum amount varies with direction." The directional structure that configuration energy depends on requires more than a scalar to specify — it requires something that captures direction-dependent behavior at each point. The natural mathematical structure for this is tensor-like, in the sense that it associates direction-dependent values with each point of the manifold.

We do not commit here to a specific tensor structure or to identifying the framework's directional content with any specific tensor of standard differential geometry. What we commit to is that the configuration energy functional has enough structure to be sensitive to directional variation in vacuum amount, and that this structure is more than a single scalar at each point. The specific mathematical form is open work.

## Flat as the Reference Configuration

The framework needs a reference configuration against which configuration energy is measured. We adopt flat vacuum as that reference.

Flat vacuum is the configuration in which vacuum amount per unit geometric extent is uniform and isotropic across all neighboring regions. There is no curvature in P3a's sense — no spatial differential of vacuum amount — and no directional variation. Every region looks like every other region; every direction at every point looks like every other direction.

Configuration energy is measured relative to flat vacuum. A flat configuration has whatever configuration energy serves as the reference value. Departures from flat — configurations with curvature — have configuration energy that differs from this reference.

We deliberately do not commit at this postulate level to flat vacuum having zero configuration energy. The reference value is whatever it is; later content can address whether the reference is at zero, at a positive baseline, or at some other value, and what consequences follow from that choice. P4's content is about the existence of configuration energy and its dependence on configuration, not about the specific value of the reference.

What we do commit to is that the reference is flat. The framework treats flat vacuum as the canonical configuration against which others are measured. This is a commitment because it is not forced — alternative reference configurations are conceivable (a uniformly curved configuration as reference, or some other choice), but the framework adopts flat as the natural reference. This is consistent with SR9's commitment that local physics in inertial frames reduces to SR's flat Minkowski structure, and with the framework's general orientation toward extending rather than replacing SR.

## What the Postulate Buys

P4 lets the framework do several things that would otherwise be unavailable.

**Total energy accounting includes vacuum configuration.** Any region of vacuum-spacetime has a specific total energy — substance energy plus configuration energy. This total can be computed (in principle, given the configuration energy functional) and tracked through physical processes. Energy bookkeeping in the framework is not just about matter and radiation; vacuum itself contributes to the energy content of any region.

**Configurations are dynamically significant.** A configuration is not just a description of how vacuum is arranged; it carries energy. This means configurations can change in response to energy-driven processes, and configuration changes can be sources or sinks of energy in interactions with other systems. The framework's later dynamical content depends on this.

**Curvature has physical content beyond geometry.** P3a defines curvature as the spatial differential of vacuum amount. P4 says this differential pattern carries energy. So curvature is not just a geometric feature of how vacuum is laid out — it is a real physical quantity with energetic content. A curved region is energetically different from a flat one.

**Directional variation has physical content.** The directional structure of vacuum amount, which P3a established as part of the framework's geometric content, is now energetically significant. Different directional patterns have different configuration energies, which means the framework can distinguish between configurations that look the same in some scalar sense but differ in their directional structure.

---

## What the Postulate Does Not Do

P4 commits to configuration energy existing and depending on directional structure. It does not commit to specifics that would require more development.

**The functional form of configuration energy is not specified.** P4 says configuration energy is a functional of the directional pattern of vacuum amount. It does not say what functional. Different functional forms would give different specific predictions, and the framework's quantitative content depends on this functional. Specifying it is open work.

**The mathematical structure of the directional content is not specified.** P4 commits to the directional content being more than a scalar but does not commit to a specific tensor structure. Whether the directional content is a (0,2) symmetric tensor (like a metric), a more general tensor, or something else is open.

**The relationship between configuration energy and other energy forms is not specified.** Configuration energy is a real physical energy, but P4 does not say how it converts to or from other energy forms. The conversion mechanisms — how kinetic energy of moving mass relates to configuration energy of the surrounding well, how radiated configuration energy carries away from a system — are addressed by later postulates and consequences.

**The dynamics of configuration energy is not specified.** P4 says configurations have energy. It does not say how configurations evolve, what drives them toward specific configurations, or what stabilizes them. Dynamics is the subject of later postulates.

**Quantitative content is not provided.** The relationship between specific curvature values and configuration energy magnitudes depends on the functional, which is unspecified. Quantitative predictions of the framework about strong-field behavior, gravitational wave energetics, and similar phenomena require pinning down the functional.

## Imports

This postulate invokes:

- SR4: Mass-Energy Equivalence (configuration energy is energy in the same sense any other energy is, contributing to the total energy of a region)
- SR9: Local Validity of SR in Inertial Frames (the flat reference configuration is the local Minkowski structure that obtains in the absence of curvature)

It depends on:

- P1: Vacuum-Energy Equivalence (the configuration is a configuration of vacuum-as-energy, so its configuration property carries energy)
- P2: Vacuum-Spacetime Identity (the configuration is the configuration of spacetime itself, not of stuff within a separate spacetime)
- P3: Vacuum Energy Density (substance energy is determined by the constant density, distinct from configuration energy)
- P3a: Spatial Differential is Curvature (the directional pattern of vacuum amount that configuration energy depends on is curvature in the framework's sense)
