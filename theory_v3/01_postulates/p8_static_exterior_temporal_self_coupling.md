# P8: Static Exterior Temporal Self-Coupling

## What This Postulate Says

In a static, source-free exterior vacuum configuration, temporal distortion is not merely a passive mapping induced by the source constraint. The temporal distortion is itself part of the vacuum configuration. Because it is part of the configuration, it contributes to the temporal mapping of the exterior configuration at the next order.

The postulate commits the framework to a specific form of this contribution in the weak-field exterior regime: temporal mappings between neighboring exterior shells compose multiplicatively. Each local temporal distortion acts on the already-distorted temporal rate inherited from the shells interior to it, rather than adding as an independent linear offset against an otherwise fixed background.

Equivalently, if the temporal scale factor is written as

$$\alpha(r) = \sqrt{-g_{tt}(r)},$$

then in a static source-free exterior weak field the logarithmic temporal response is fixed, through the order needed for one-post-Newtonian recovery, by the first-order potential difference alone:

$$d\ln \alpha = -\frac{dU}{c^2} + O(c^{-6}).$$

Here $U(r)$ is the positive Newtonian potential magnitude, with $U \to 0$ at infinity and larger $U$ deeper in the well. The absence of an independent $U^2/c^4$ term in $\ln\alpha$ is the postulate's second-order content: the second-order temporal coefficient comes from multiplicative composition of the first-order distortion, not from a separate scalar-metric ansatz.

This postulate is not a full field equation. It does not determine the source law for $U(r)$. It does not determine the spatial metric. It does not claim that all gravitational geometry is generated by one scalar potential. It is a restricted structural commitment about temporal self-coupling in static, source-free exterior weak fields.

## The Question This Answers

T1 derives gravitational redshift in the weak-field limit. T2 derives gravitational time dilation from that redshift relation. Together, they establish the first-order temporal mapping between observers at different positions in a curvature gradient.

T3, using P7, establishes reciprocal exterior scaling in static source-free spherical exteriors:

$$A(r)B(r)=1.$$

That result supplies the spatial-response side of weak-field recovery. In PPN language, it gives the framework's analogue of

$$\gamma = 1.$$

But weak-field recovery of general relativity requires more than the first-order temporal mapping and the first-order spatial response. The one-post-Newtonian temporal sector also contains a nonlinear self-coupling coefficient. In PPN language this is the parameter $\beta$.

The framework needs to recover

$$\beta = 1$$

without smuggling in a scalar metric ansatz. A tempting route would be to declare a full exponential scalar metric, such as

$$g_{tt} = -e^{-2U/c^2}, \qquad g_{ij} = e^{2U/c^2}\delta_{ij}.$$

That route gives the desired coefficients algebraically, but it risks collapsing the framework's directional, tensor-like vacuum structure into a single scalar response. This is not acceptable as a general framework move. P3a and P4 require curvature and configuration energy to have directional structure; the vacuum cannot be reduced globally to one scalar potential controlling all metric components.

P8 answers the narrower question: what structural temporal rule is needed in static source-free exteriors so that the framework recovers the correct second-order temporal coefficient, while avoiding a universal scalar-metric postulate?

The answer is temporal self-coupling by multiplicative composition.

## Why This Has to Be a Postulate

P1 through P6 motivate temporal self-coupling, but they do not derive its exact weak-field form.

P1 identifies vacuum with energy. P2 identifies vacuum with spacetime. P4 says vacuum configurations carry energy. P5 says the vacuum relaxes toward minimum-energy configurations under constraints. These commitments make it natural to treat temporal distortion as physically real configuration content, not as a coordinate label imposed from outside.

But those commitments do not, by themselves, fix the second-order temporal coefficient. They tell us that configuration matters and that exterior configurations should be dynamically meaningful. They do not specify the field equation or configuration-energy functional from which the second-order temporal mapping could be computed.

P7 has the same kind of status in the spatial sector. The earlier postulates motivate a distinction between substance exchange and configuration redistribution, but they do not force the specific exterior compensation condition. P7 therefore adopts a restricted exterior structural principle, and T3 derives the reciprocal scaling result from it.

P8 plays the analogous role in the temporal nonlinear sector. It adopts a restricted exterior structural principle: temporal mappings in static source-free exterior weak fields compose multiplicatively because temporal distortion is part of the vacuum configuration and therefore acts on already-distorted temporal rates.

A future version of the framework may derive P8 from a deeper configuration-energy functional or field equation. Until then, P8 stands as a localized recovery postulate: it states a condition that any successful future field equation must reproduce in the appropriate exterior weak-field limit.

## Temporal Distortion as Configuration Content

The framework does not treat gravitational time dilation as a bare coordinate convention. T2 derives time dilation from gravitational redshift, and the physical interpretation is that clock-rate mappings reflect differences in vacuum structure between locations.

Under P1 and P2, the vacuum is both energy and spacetime. Under P3a, curvature is differential vacuum extent. Under P4, curvature configurations carry energy. A temporal mapping difference in a gravitational exterior is therefore a real aspect of the vacuum configuration.

This matters for second-order behavior.

If temporal distortion were merely a passive label, then first-order time dilation would be all the temporal sector contributes unless some additional field equation supplied nonlinear terms. But if temporal distortion is configuration content, then an already-existing temporal distortion affects how additional temporal distortions compose. A local shell does not map time against an undistorted background; it maps time relative to the temporal scale already accumulated along the exterior configuration.

This is the core of P8.

Temporal mapping is not additive:

$$\alpha_{\text{total}} \neq 1 + \sum_i \delta_i$$

as though every shell contributed a small offset against a fixed background.

Temporal mapping is multiplicative:

$$\alpha_{\text{total}} = \prod_i (1+\delta_i),$$

because each shell maps the rate delivered to it by the previous shell. In the continuum limit, multiplicative composition becomes a logarithmic differential rule:

$$d\ln \alpha = -\frac{dU}{c^2}.$$

The first-order effect is the same as the T1/T2 temporal mapping. The difference appears at second order, where multiplicative composition makes the first-order distortion self-couple.

## The Postulate

**P8 (Static Exterior Temporal Self-Coupling).** In a static, source-free exterior vacuum configuration, temporal distortion is part of the vacuum configuration rather than a passive mapping imposed on it. Temporal mappings between neighboring exterior shells therefore compose multiplicatively: each local temporal distortion acts on the already-distorted temporal scale inherited from the surrounding exterior configuration.

In the weak-field exterior regime, this means that for the temporal scale factor

$$\alpha(r)=\sqrt{-g_{tt}(r)},$$

the logarithmic temporal response obeys, through one-post-Newtonian order,

$$d\ln \alpha = -\frac{dU}{c^2}+O(c^{-6}),$$

where $U(r)$ is the positive Newtonian potential magnitude, with $U \to 0$ at spatial infinity. Equivalently, $\ln\alpha$ has no independent quadratic term proportional to $U^2/c^4$ in this exterior weak-field expansion.

This postulate applies only to static, source-free exterior weak fields. It does not define a universal scalar metric, does not specify the spatial response, and does not replace the future field equation. It is a structural constraint on the temporal sector that any future field equation must reproduce in this regime.

## Relationship to P7

P7 and P8 are parallel exterior recovery postulates, but they do different work.

P7 says that static source-free exterior curvature is compensated temporal-radial redistribution rather than net vacuum substance exchange. T3 then derives

$$A(r)B(r)=1,$$

which gives the framework's weak-field analogue of

$$\gamma=1.$$

P8 says that static source-free exterior temporal distortion self-couples by multiplicative composition. The corresponding theorem derives the second-order temporal coefficient, giving the framework's weak-field analogue of

$$\beta=1.$$

Thus:

$$\gamma=1$$

comes from exterior temporal-radial compensation, while

$$\beta=1$$

comes from exterior temporal self-coupling.

The two are related because both concern static source-free exterior vacuum behavior, but neither should be collapsed into the other. P7 is not a statement about second-order temporal nonlinearity. P8 is not a statement about radial spatial compensation. Keeping them separate prevents the framework from hiding multiple recovery conditions inside a single scalar metric ansatz.

## Relationship to the Scalar-Metric Failure Mode

A previous route toward weak-field recovery attempted to unify the temporal and spatial weak-field coefficients through a single scalar exponential ansatz. Algebraically, that route is attractive: one can write temporal and spatial scale factors as reciprocal exponentials and obtain both $\gamma=1$ and $\beta=1$.

The problem is that such an ansatz can define the whole geometry from one scalar function. If promoted beyond its restricted static exterior use, it risks turning the framework into a scalar theory of gravity.

That is not compatible with the current postulate set.

P3a defines curvature through spatial differentials of vacuum amount, with directional structure. P4 says configuration energy depends on the directional pattern of vacuum amount, not merely on a scalar field. A complete vacuum geometry must therefore be capable of representing tensor-like or direction-dependent structure. It cannot be globally reduced to a scalar potential without losing the framework's intended ontology.

P8 avoids this failure mode by being narrower.

It does not say:

$$\alpha=e^{-U/c^2}$$

as an exact universal metric law.

It says that in static, source-free exterior weak fields, temporal mappings compose multiplicatively through the order needed for one-post-Newtonian recovery. The exponential appears only as the second-order integrated form of the local temporal composition rule in this restricted regime, and the theorem using P8 needs only its expansion through $O(c^{-4})$ in $-g_{tt}$.

The spatial response remains governed separately by P7/T3 and, in the full theory, by the future configuration-energy functional or field equation.

## What P8 Buys

P8 supplies the missing temporal nonlinear recovery condition for the framework's weak-field program.

T1 and T2 give the first-order temporal mapping. T3 gives the reciprocal radial-spatial response and therefore closes the $\gamma=1$ side of weak-field recovery. What remains is the second-order temporal coefficient required for one-post-Newtonian agreement with general relativity.

P8 supplies the structural rule from which that coefficient follows.

Given P8, the temporal scale factor obeys

$$d\ln\alpha=-\frac{dU}{c^2}+O(c^{-6}).$$

With asymptotic flatness,

$$\alpha\to1$$

as

$$U\to0,$$

so integration gives, in the exterior weak-field regime through the needed order,

$$\ln\alpha=-\frac{U}{c^2}+O(c^{-6}).$$

Equivalently, through second order,

$$\alpha=1-\frac{U}{c^2}+\frac{1}{2}\frac{U^2}{c^4}+O(c^{-6}).$$

Since

$$-g_{tt}=\alpha^2,$$

we get

$$-g_{tt}=1-\frac{2U}{c^2}+2\frac{U^2}{c^4}+O(c^{-6}).$$

Comparing with the PPN temporal form,

$$-g_{tt}=1-\frac{2U}{c^2}+2\beta\frac{U^2}{c^4}+O(c^{-6}),$$

this gives

$$\beta=1.$$

This derivation belongs in the theorem downstream of P8, not in the postulate itself. The postulate supplies the temporal composition rule; the theorem extracts the PPN consequence.

## What P8 Does Not Commit To

P8 is deliberately narrow.

It does not commit to a full Schwarzschild solution. It fixes the second-order temporal coefficient in the static source-free exterior weak-field expansion. It does not derive the exact strong-field form of $g_{tt}$.

It does not specify the source law. The function $U(r)$ is treated as the exterior Newtonian potential magnitude in the weak-field limit. P8 does not derive $U=GM/r$ or any deeper relation between mass constraints and exterior potential profiles.

It does not determine the spatial metric. The radial spatial response is governed by P7/T3 in the static spherical exterior case. More general spatial structure remains future field-equation work.

It does not apply to cosmology. Cosmic expansion is not a static source-free exterior configuration; it is a substance-regime process involving vacuum/spacetime creation.

It does not apply inside matter. Inside sources, the exterior source-free assumptions fail.

It does not apply automatically to rotating, radiating, time-dependent, or nonspherical configurations. Those regimes require the future field equation or additional structural commitments.

It does not make the framework scalar. The temporal exponential form that appears after integration is a restricted consequence of the temporal composition rule in the static weak-field exterior. It is not a claim that all metric components are generated by one scalar potential.

## Epistemic Status

P8 is not derived from P1–P7.

P1–P6 make temporal self-coupling natural by treating vacuum/spacetime configuration as physically real energy-bearing structure. P7 shows how a restricted exterior compensation principle can be adopted without collapsing the framework into a full metric ansatz. T1 and T2 expose the first-order temporal mapping that P8 must be consistent with. But none of these existing commitments logically forces the multiplicative temporal composition rule or the absence of an independent quadratic term in $\ln\alpha$.

P8 is therefore a new structural postulate.

Its role is to localize the second-order temporal recovery condition in the same way P7 localizes the reciprocal exterior spatial recovery condition. The framework's eventual field equation should make P8 redundant by deriving its temporal self-coupling rule from the configuration-energy functional. Until that deeper derivation exists, P8 stands as a precise constraint on acceptable field-equation design.

The framework's position is not that $\beta=1$ has been derived from the original ontology alone. The position is that the original ontology motivates a particular temporal self-coupling rule in static source-free exteriors, and adopting that rule yields the required second-order temporal coefficient without imposing a universal scalar metric.

## Summary

P8 states that static source-free exterior temporal distortion self-couples by multiplicative composition.

Temporal distortion is part of the vacuum configuration, not a passive label. Therefore local temporal mappings act on already-distorted temporal rates. In the weak-field exterior regime this gives, through the order needed for one-post-Newtonian recovery,

$$d\ln\alpha=-\frac{dU}{c^2}+O(c^{-6}),$$

where

$$\alpha=\sqrt{-g_{tt}}.$$

The downstream theorem expands this relation through second order and obtains

$$-g_{tt}=1-\frac{2U}{c^2}+2\frac{U^2}{c^4}+O(c^{-6}),$$

which gives

$$\beta=1.$$

P8 is the temporal nonlinear counterpart to P7. P7 supplies the exterior compensation principle that leads to $\gamma=1$. P8 supplies the exterior temporal self-coupling principle that leads to $\beta=1$.

Both are localized structural commitments that future field equations must reproduce. Neither is a full field equation, and neither should be used to collapse the framework into a scalar metric theory.
