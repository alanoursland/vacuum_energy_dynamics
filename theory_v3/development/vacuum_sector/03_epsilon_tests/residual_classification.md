# Residual Classification

Residuals should be classified before they are interpreted.

## Primary Buckets

```text
epsilon = 0 equivalent
```

The term is physically inert in the relevant sector, removable by field
redefinition, boundary-equivalent, topological, or otherwise does not change
the field equations.

```text
controlled epsilon != 0
```

The term survives the accumulated gates, has explicit equations, and has
testable consequences or a clearly routed observable channel.

```text
fails accumulated gate
```

The term violates metric limit, diffeomorphism/relabeling invariance,
conservation, boundary differentiability, hyperbolicity, mode count,
weak-field constraints, or source-ledger purity without an explicit route.

```text
underdetermined without new axiom
```

The current ontology cannot decide whether the term is allowed, killed, or
selected.

```text
not yet evaluated
```

The term has not been passed through the manifest.

## Residual Types

Candidate residuals should also identify their type:

```text
boundary/topological term
field redefinition
higher-curvature term
higher-derivative term
nonmetric/torsion term
Finsler/nonquadratic term
medium/elastic term
nonlocal/relaxation term
baseline/Lambda term
dark-sector excess term
non-gravitational channel term
```

## Classification Rule

Do not use `controlled epsilon != 0` until the branch has:

```text
an X contract;
a neighboring mismatch contract;
a boundary rule;
a conservation identity or explicit failure route;
a mode-count route;
a weak-field residual result;
a kill condition.
```
