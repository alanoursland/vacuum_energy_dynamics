# 73_layer_generator_construction — Plan

## Purpose

Group 73 attempts the narrow constructive route left open by Group 72.

Group 72 showed that `D_layer` cannot be supplied by the old diagnostic transition response, cannot carry source/trace/mass payload, and cannot act as repair. It retained only a possible geometric boundary-layer component route:

```text
D_layer may be a legitimate boundary component only if it is derived from boundary/layer geometry.
```

Group 73 asks whether such a geometric layer generator can be constructed, or whether the layer route remains only a scaffold/theorem target.

This is not a parent-equation construction group.

```text
73_layer_generator_construction
```

```text
Can D_layer be derived from a legitimate geometric layer generator, rather than from diagnostic transition material, free coefficients, source/trace payload, or repair cancellation?
```

A useful result would be one of these:

### Route A: Geometric Layer Generator Constructed

```text
a signed-distance / boundary-measure / layer-flux object is derived;
D_layer is produced as a boundary-layer component;
D_layer carries no source, trace, mass, repair, active-O, or diagnostic-transition payload;
component anti-match can remain meaningful;
parent divergence remains blocked unless lift neutrality also closes.
```

```text

but component origin, amplitude, covariant lift, or payload purity remains unproved.

Status:
  D_layer route retained only as theorem target / controlled obstruction.
```

### Route C: Generator Not Derived

```text
simple geometric layer-generator candidates fail to derive D_layer without manual payload or repair.

Status:
  D_layer route remains unresolved or is sent to route-management / split-theorem decision.
```

## Script Intent

### 1. candidate_layer_generator_problem.py

Open Group 73 as a geometric layer-generator construction attempt.

### 2. candidate_geometric_layer_generator_requirements.py

Convert Group 72 obligations into legal generator requirements.

A legal generator must:

```text
be geometric before coefficient choice;
be local to the boundary/layer region;
supply D_layer as a boundary component;
exclude diagnostic transition payload;
exclude source/trace/mass payload;
exclude repair and active-O payload;
connect to common boundary object if later used in boundary-lift matching.
```

### 3. candidate_signed_distance_layer_scaffold.py

Test a signed-distance / layer-coordinate scaffold.

This may show that layer-local support can be represented, but it must not call that physical legitimacy.

### 4. candidate_boundary_measure_origin_test.py

Test whether a boundary/layer measure can supply a legitimate support or neutrality structure.

This should distinguish:

```text
measure scaffold;
weighted neutrality condition;
physical D_layer origin.
```

### 5. candidate_component_membership_origin_test.py

Test whether jump/layer/tail can be members of one boundary generator.

Special attention: `D_layer` membership must be derived, not assumed.

### 6. candidate_payload_purity_and_role_test.py

Test source, trace, mass, repair, diagnostic, and active-O payload flags.

Legal route requires all forbidden payload flags vanish.

### 7. candidate_boundary_lift_interface_test.py

Test whether a legitimate geometric D_layer would interact with the Group 70/71 boundary-lift anti-match.

This should preserve that `L_bulk=0` and `L_gauge=0` are still separate lift-cleanliness obligations.

### 8. candidate_layer_generator_route_classifier.py

Classify the final status:
