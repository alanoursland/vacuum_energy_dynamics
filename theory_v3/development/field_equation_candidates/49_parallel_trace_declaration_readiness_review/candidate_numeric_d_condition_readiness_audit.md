# Candidate Numeric d Condition Readiness Audit — Result

## Result

This script decides that numeric `d` does not block a future parallel declaration attempt, provided the attempt remains symbolic and scope-conditioned. That is one of the most important Group 49 conclusions.

## Main Findings

Symbolic `d` may be carried as the traced-dimension field in a paired declaration attempt. Numeric `d` remains explicitly conditioned and is not fixed here.

This means the future declaration attempt does not need to solve the numeric-d problem before it can be written. It must instead preserve the numeric-d condition as a visible clause.

The factor-of-two burden remains protected. Dimension handling cannot be used to make `2*zeta/d` and `zeta/d` look equivalent, and numeric `d` cannot be chosen from recovery, algebraic aesthetics, or parent-facing convenience.

## Boundary

This is not numeric dimension closure. It does not set spatial `d=3`, does not assign a parent-facing dimension, and does not convert symbolic review `d` into a physical scope.

## Steering Conclusion

The numeric-d issue has been demoted from a declaration blocker to a declaration condition. The surviving future route is a symbolic paired declaration attempt with numeric `d` still conditioned.
