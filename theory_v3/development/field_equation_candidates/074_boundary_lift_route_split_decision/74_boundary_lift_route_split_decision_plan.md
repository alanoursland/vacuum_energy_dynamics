# 74_boundary_lift_route_split_decision — Plan

## Purpose

Group 74 performs route management after Groups 71–73.

Group 71 did not establish a strong common boundary generator in the tested broad symbolic/governance classes. It retained the boundary-lift matching route only as a partial theorem target and split the remaining burdens into:

```text
orientation/component anti-match compatibility;
D_layer legitimacy;
L_bulk / L_gauge lift cleanliness.
```

Group 72 audited `D_layer` legitimacy and rejected diagnostic transition insertion. It retained a geometric layer component only as a theorem target.

Group 73 attempted a constructive geometric `D_layer` generator route. It produced signed-distance and boundary-measure scaffolds, but did not derive a physical/covariant layer generator. It left component membership, payload purity, and lift cleanliness as theorem burdens.

Group 74 should decide how the boundary-lift route should now be carried forward without pretending that the route closed.

This is not a parent-equation construction group. It is not active-O construction. It is not `B_s/F_zeta` insertion. It is route governance after controlled obstruction.
