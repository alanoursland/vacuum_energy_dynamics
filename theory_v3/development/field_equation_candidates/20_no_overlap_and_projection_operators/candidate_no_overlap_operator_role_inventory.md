## Rejected Branches

The recovery-defined projector branch is rejected.

Recovery targets such as:

```text
gamma_like,
AB,
Schwarzschild behavior,
PPN behavior
```

may test a projector after it exists. They may not define its kernel, image, support, coefficients, or residual status.

The decorative projector branch is also rejected.

A real projector must specify at least:

```text
domain,
codomain,
kernel,
image,
composition / idempotence law,
measure or pairing if orthogonality is claimed,
boundary behavior.
```

Without those, no-overlap language is only a label.

## Failure Controls

No-overlap inventory fails if:

1. \(O\) is defined as whatever prevents overlap.
2. \(O\) erases residual trace after the fact.
3. \(O\) makes \(H_{\rm curv}\) or \(H_{\rm exch}\) insertable.
4. \(O\) hides ordinary matter double-counting.
5. \(O\) chooses \(B_s/F_\zeta\) by recovery.
6. \(O\) separates \(\Sigma/R\) or \(J_{\rm sub}/J_{\rm exch}\) by name.
7. \(O\) cancels boundary leakage or scalar charge.
8. \(O\) protects \(M_{\rm ext}\) by declaration.
9. \(O\) has no domain / kernel / image / idempotence burden.
10. Orthogonality is claimed without measure or pairing.
