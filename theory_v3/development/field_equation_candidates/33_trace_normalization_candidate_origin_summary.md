## Structural Trace Origin

The volume-trace ledger considered \(\zeta\) as a logarithmic spatial volume trace over a traced sector of dimension \(d\).

If \(\zeta\) is total volume-log trace, then the uniform scale-factor convention gives:

\[
q = \frac{\zeta}{d}.
\]

If \(B_s\) is treated as a spatial scale factor, the candidate form is:

\[
\log(B_s)=\frac{\zeta}{d}.
\]

If \(B_s\) is treated as a spatial metric coefficient response, the candidate form is:

\[
\log(B_s)=\frac{2\zeta}{d}.
\]

The factor of two is not a recovery tune. It is the ordinary distinction between a scale factor and a metric coefficient.

A first-order trace identity may be useful, but it is not an exact nonlinear coefficient law.
