# candidate_mass_filter — Result Note

## Result

The mass filter applies A-sector mass-neutrality protection to insertion families.

It computes:

```text
Delta_M = Q_trace*alpha
Delta_M|Q_trace=0 = 0
```

The nonzero trace-mass route is rejected.

## Main Findings

A nonzero independent trace-sector mass charge shifts exterior mass. That violates A-sector mass protection.

The zero-charge route survives only conditionally:

```text
Q_trace=0
```

or equivalently a later theorem that the trace-sector payload is inert or non-mass-carrying.

The script rejects three shortcuts:

```text
absorbing the shift into M_ext;
renaming Q_trace as a dark contribution;
assuming Q_trace=0 without support.
```

## Boundary

This is not a mass-neutrality theorem. It does not prove `Q_trace=0` and does not license insertion.

## Steering Consequence

Mass-shifting insertion routes are excluded. Any surviving insertion route must preserve trace-sector mass neutrality.
