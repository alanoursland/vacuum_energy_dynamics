## Result

The route classifier gives the main Group 52 classification: the conditional trace-normalization candidate survives only as audit material and remains blocked for physical use.

## Main Findings

```text

  burden explicit; single-entry theorem open

residual nonentry:
  residual metric/source reentry rejected or theorem-burdened

  source duplication witnesses block physical use without routing theorem
```

This is a clear non-looping result. Group 52 did not merely repeat “safety missing.” It found and organized the actual ways safety can fail.

The classifier also correctly avoids overclaiming. Diagnostic witnesses do not kill the candidate entirely, because the candidate can still be useful as audit material. But those witnesses do block physical use until the relevant safety theorems are supplied.

## Boundary

The classifier is not a safety proof. It does not open insertion, adoption, active `O`, recombination, or parent closure. It also does not force immediate active `O`; `O` necessity remains a separate audit after non-`O` safety routes are tested.

## Steering Consequence

The best next work is focused theorem work, probably residual/source safety or boundary/scalar-silence. The next group should not loop back to trace declaration and should not jump straight to active `O` unless a specific obstruction forces that audit.
