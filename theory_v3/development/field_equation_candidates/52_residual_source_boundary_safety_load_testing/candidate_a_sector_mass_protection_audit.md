# candidate_a_sector_mass_protection_audit — Result Note

## Result

The A-sector mass protection audit confirms the protected reduced mass coin and shows why independent scalar trace charge is dangerous.

For the reduced exterior solution,

```text
A_ext = 1 - 2GM/(c^2 r)
```

the script computes:

```text
F_A = 4*pi*r^2*A' = 8*pi*G*M/c^2
M_A = c^2*F_A/(8*pi*G) = M
M_A - M = 0
```

Then it adds an independent trace-sector charge `Q_trace` and finds:

```text
M_effective - M = Q_trace
```

## Main Findings

The strongest reduced result remains intact: the A-sector areal-flux mass coin recovers `M` in the reduced static spherical exterior.

But the diagnostic also shows the threat. If a trace-sector contribution acts as an independent long-range mass charge, the effective mass shifts by `Q_trace`. That would duplicate or alter the protected A-sector mass response.

This means `B_s`, `zeta`, `kappa`, residual variables, and boundary terms must not become independent mass channels unless a separate theorem neutralizes or forbids the extra charge.

## Boundary

This audit protects a reduced exterior result. It is not a covariant mass theorem. It does not prove trace-sector mass neutrality. It shows what must be protected.

## Steering Consequence

A-sector mass protection is now a named safety theorem target. Any trace-normalization route that gives `zeta`, `B_s`, residual variables, or boundary terms an independent mass charge is blocked for physical use unless that charge is proven zero, inert, or non-mass-carrying.
