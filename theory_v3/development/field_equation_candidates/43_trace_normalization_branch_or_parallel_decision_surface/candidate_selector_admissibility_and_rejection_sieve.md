# Candidate Selector Admissibility and Rejection Sieve

## Result

This script classified branch-decision selectors into forbidden selectors, admissible context, and explicit-choice requirements.

It did not activate any selector and did not choose the metric branch, scale branch, or parallel-record route.

## Main Findings

Recovery selectors are rejected. `AB=1`, `B=1/A`, Schwarzschild recovery, PPN gamma, weak-field success, `kappa=0`, and parent-fit success cannot construct or select trace normalization.

Downstream convenience selectors are also rejected. The project cannot choose a branch because it makes insertion, residual handling, parent fit, or later algebra easier.

Neutral-expression selectors are rejected because neutral `F_zeta` must remain expression-free. Symbol-shape and majority-hit selectors are rejected as standalone support because notation count without evidence hierarchy is too weak.

The script allows two kinds of context: source hierarchy may inform a later decision if evidence quality is ranked, and branch-consequence audits may expose obligations or risks. Theory-owner convention choice may also be admissible, but only if explicitly labeled as choice rather than derivation.

## Boundary

Context is not selection. Intuition is not derivation. A source hierarchy is not a theorem. Consequence burden is not a downstream-convenience selector.

The main protection is that any future branch choice must happen in daylight as an explicit record, not through recovery, convenience, neutral notation, or prose drift.
