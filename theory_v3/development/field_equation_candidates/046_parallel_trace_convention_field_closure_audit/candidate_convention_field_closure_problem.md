# Candidate Convention Field Closure Problem

## Result

The script opens Group 46 as a convention-field closure audit for the explicit parallel trace-normalization records produced in Group 45.

The important result is that Group 46 is allowed to sharpen or close shared fields for **record review**, but not for active trace normalization. The group may classify the open fields left by the metric and scale records—`zeta` convention, traced dimension `d`, normalization scope, record status, branch-pair domain, and handoff conditions—without choosing `B_s_metric` or `b_s_scale`.

## Main Findings

- Convention fields may be attacked directly instead of left as vague “not ready” gaps.
- Field closure is allowed only at the pre-declaration record-review level.
- Unclosed fields should be classified as axiom-required, choice-required, theorem-required, or deferred with a named target.
- The metric and scale records must remain explicitly paired, branch-labeled, expression-separated, and non-active.
- Recovery, downstream convenience, neutral `F_zeta`, and parent-fit pressure remain forbidden convention sources.

## Interpretation

This opener usefully changes the work from broad readiness auditing to targeted convention-field pressure. The key steering move is the demand that open fields become named blockers rather than background uncertainty.

Group 46 therefore should not end by saying “trace normalization is still not ready.” It should say which convention fields are closed for review and which specific field blocks declaration.

## Boundary

This is not branch choice, not trace-normalization declaration, not Package B adoption, and not insertion support. Closing or classifying a convention field cannot activate either branch or make the parallel records field-equation machinery.
