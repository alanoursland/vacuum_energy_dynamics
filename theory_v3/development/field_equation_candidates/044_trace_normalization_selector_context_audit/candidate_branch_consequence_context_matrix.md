# Candidate Branch Consequence Context Matrix

## Result

This script compared the consequences of the metric, scale, parallel-record, and continued-deferral routes as context only.

The main result is that consequence profiles can sharpen the decision surface by exposing risks and obligations, but they still cannot choose the route. In particular, ease of insertion, algebraic neatness, or downstream convenience cannot decide the branch.

## Main Findings

- The metric route keeps `log(B_s_metric)=2*zeta/d` visible and carries the factor-of-two burden explicitly under the metric-coefficient interpretation.
- The scale route keeps `log(b_s_scale)=zeta/d` visible and may align with volume/root intuition, but that intuition remains context rather than derivation.
- The parallel-record route reduces hidden-branch risk by carrying both branch-indexed forms, but it is not one neutral law or a final theory state.
- Continued deferral can be productive if it allows residual/source/boundary theorem work to narrow the choice later.
- All routes remain distant from `B_s/F_zeta` insertion because residual/source/boundary/divergence gates still control insertion readiness.
- Hidden-branch risk is lowest when branch labels remain explicit.

## Boundary

Consequence comparison is not downstream-convenience selection. It cannot choose the route that looks easiest to insert, cleanest algebraically, or most convenient for a future parent equation.

This script does not choose metric, scale, parallel, or deferral. It does not complete trace normalization and does not open insertion.

## Steering Note

The strongest practical signal here is that visibility itself is valuable. Parallel records and explicit branch labels reduce hidden-choice risk. But that does not mean parallel records solve trace normalization; it means they may be the safest technical posture if the project is not ready to make a labeled convention choice.
