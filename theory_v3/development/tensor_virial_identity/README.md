# Tensor-Virial Identity

## Purpose

This directory holds the rigor upgrade for the tensor-virial identity
used in the radiative bootstrap's quadrupole chain.

Current status: completed. See `tensor_virial_identity_note.md` and the
forge script
`vacuum_forge/src/field_equation_trials/014_tensor_virial_identity/tensor_virial_identity_general.py`.

## Scope

The 008 radiative bootstrap already derives the radiative normalization
and quadrupole coefficient, but it records one narrow debt: the
tensor-virial step was checked there on a compact one-dimensional
witness rather than stated and discharged in generality.

The identity needed in the quadrupole chain is

$$
\int T^{ij}\,d^3x
=
{1\over 2c^2}{d^2\over dt^2}
\int T^{00}x^i x^j\,d^3x .
$$

This note does not add physics and cannot move a coefficient. It is a
flat-background conservation identity.

## Assumptions

The general identity requires:

1. flat-background stress conservation in coordinates \(x^0=ct\),
   \(\partial_\mu T^{\mu\nu}=0\);
2. symmetric stress tensor, \(T^{ij}=T^{ji}\);
3. compact support or falloff strong enough to kill the two surface
   terms generated by integration by parts;
4. enough differentiability to commute the time derivatives through the
   spatial integral.

These are exactly the assumptions used by the standard quadrupole
derivation in the slow-source, far-zone regime.
