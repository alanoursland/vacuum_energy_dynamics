# Negative Results

The negative results are among the strongest products of the project.

They show which routes cannot work, and they prevent the scalar discovery from being overpromoted.

## 1. Scalar trace data cannot recover traceless tensor data

A scalar trace channel can see only one component. It cannot reconstruct shear, off-diagonal metric data, or the traceless sector.

This is a rank/data-content limitation.

## 2. Scalar boundary flux cannot encode TT radiation

The scalar boundary flux ledger captures monopole / constraint / Coulombic data.

TT radiation can carry nonzero energy and memory while having zero scalar trace and no scalar monopole change.

Thus:

```text
scalar boundary charge != radiative tensor flux.
```

## 3. Scalar nonlinear dressing cannot reproduce GR post-Newtonian tensor structure

Adding scalar nonlinearities to the Newtonian sector does not generate the missing tensor structures of GR.

Scalar expressions such as

```text
Phi, Phi^2, (grad Phi)^2, ...
```

remain scalar constructions. They do not produce independent shear, vector, TT, or Weyl degrees of freedom.

This closes the loophole:

```text
Maybe scalar nonlinear corrections can fake GR.
```

They cannot, unless non-scalar data is imported.

## 4. Trace-only/conformal coupling misses traceless stress

A conformal scalar response sees the trace of stress but misses traceless stress, including null radiation-like cases.

Full matter coupling requires full metric variation, not only scalar/conformal response.

## 5. Stress tensor coupling does not derive matter ontology

If matter uses the shared metric interval, stress coupling follows.

But the stress tensor does not derive:

```text
matter existence,
species count,
mass spectrum,
charge,
spin,
internal symmetry,
quantization,
particle number,
localization.
```

Matter ontology remains open.

## 6. Finite scalar flux does not determine the projection contact index R

Weak-field GR scalar data fixes a finite-flux `1/r` exterior class.

It does not by itself choose the compactified moment pairing or the projection index `R`.

`R` belongs to the projection embedding.

## 7. Nonquadratic response cannot be hidden inside the metric branch

If response is nonquadratic, polarization does not produce a single direction-independent bilinear metric.

Nonquadratic residuals must be routed explicitly as Finsler, medium, or constitutive structures.

## 8. `r_k` alone does not derive EH/GHY

The original coefficient is a scalar boundary/admissibility object.

It does not by itself derive the Einstein-Hilbert action, the GHY boundary term, matter coupling, Weyl dynamics, or nonlinear GR.

## Why these negative results matter

They make the project stronger.

They show that the scalar origin story is real but limited, and they force the transition to directional/tensor geometry and eventually to a vacuum strain functional.

The negative ledger says:

```text
Do not look for the full theory inside the scalar ladder.
Look for the full theory in the strain/transport law that produces the scalar ladder as one boundary shadow.
```
