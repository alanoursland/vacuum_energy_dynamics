# The Scalar Trace Sector

The scalar projection ladder captures the trace / monopole / Newtonian boundary sector.

It does not capture the whole gravitational field.

The scalar ladder sees data of the following kind:

```text
scalar moment kernels,
monopole flux,
finite Gauss charge,
Newtonian 1/r exterior behavior,
trace-like source coupling,
compactified endpoint admissibility.
```

This is why it aligns naturally with weak-field Newtonian gravity. In the slow-motion weak-field limit, the gravitational field reduces to a scalar potential satisfying a Poisson equation:

```text
Delta Phi = 4 pi G rho.
```

The exterior solution has finite flux and `1/r` falloff. That scalar boundary ledger is the natural home of the original projection coefficient.

But full GR contains much more than this scalar sector.

The scalar ladder cannot recover:

```text
traceless shear,
off-diagonal tensor response,
Weyl curvature,
transverse-traceless radiation,
Bondi news or memory,
boundary symplectic phase space,
full matter stress structure,
nonlinear constraint closure.
```

The clean slogan is:

```text
Newtonian gravity is the scalar weak-field constraint sector.
Full GR requires the traceless tensor sector as well.
```

This should not be overstated as a literal identity between Newtonian gravity and a tensor trace. The precise claim is that the Newtonian limit is a scalar/constraint/monopole reduction of the full tensor theory.

The scalar trace sector is valuable because it is where the original `r_k` lives. It is also limited because scalar data is intrinsically rank-deficient relative to metric data.

A symmetric tensor on an `m`-dimensional boundary has

```text
m(m+1)/2
```

components. A scalar trace channel supplies only one. The missing components include traceless shear and other directional/tensor information.

Thus the scalar ladder should be protected from two opposite mistakes:

```text
Do not dismiss it as numerology.
Do not promote it into the whole gravitational field.
```

Its proper role is:

```text
scalar admissibility ladder = trace / monopole / Newtonian boundary shadow.
```
