# Assumption Ledger Final Audit — Proof Chain Plan

## Purpose

This folder is a consolidation/audit layer, not a new physics-origin claim.
It records what the preceding proof groups actually closed, what they closed
only under stated assumptions, and what remains imported or open.

The key discipline is:

```text
closed locally != derived globally
conditional closure != ontology-level derivation
projection compatibility != physical identification
```

## Strategy

Each script validates one ledger relation using symbolic/counting checks, then
writes a Markdown report. The goal is to prevent two opposite errors:

1. underclaiming solved results, especially the `r_k` boundary/admissibility
   origin and scalar trace-sector limitation;
2. overclaiming conditional bridges as full derivations of GR, matter, or
   spacetime ontology.

## Batches

### Batch A — closed scalar/boundary results

- `r_k` is the `R=0` moment-kernel/contact coefficient.
- The scalar ladder is a trace/monopole/Newtonian boundary sector.
- `R_GR` is not specified by weak-field GR scalar finite-flux data alone; it
  requires a projection embedding.

### Batch B — conditional metric/geometric bridges

- exact quadratic/parallelogram response is the metric gate;
- directional probes reconstruct symmetric tensor data;
- scalar trace data has a rank gap relative to full boundary tensor data;
- Weyl/TT/radiative sectors are outside the scalar ledger.

### Batch C — source and action ledgers

- shared metric interval dependence implies stress coupling;
- stress coupling does not derive matter ontology;
- EH/GHY and nonlinear constraint closure remain conditional on metric,
  diffeomorphism, locality, and no-extra-field assumptions.

### Batch D — final status tables

- classify claims as closed, conditional, open, or negative/no-go;
- identify recommended frontier groups.

## Expected result

A stable ledger:

```text
Closed: scalar projection/admissibility and scalar limitation results.
Conditional: metric, stress, EH/GHY, torsion, dimension, Lambda branches.
Open: exact physical origin of quadratic response, matter ontology, Weyl-sector
      origin, radiative nonlinear completion, quantum structure.
```
