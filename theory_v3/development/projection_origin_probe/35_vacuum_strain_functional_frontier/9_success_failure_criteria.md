# 9. Success and Failure Criteria

This document defines what would count as progress in the next phase.

## Success A: exact GR recovery

The accumulated vacuum principles force:

```text
K_strain = K_EH/GHY
```

up to boundary-equivalent terms and constants.

Then:

```text
ε = 0.
```

This would be a strong rederivation of classical GR from the vacuum ontology.

It would not be a new-prediction theory, but it would be a real assumption-reduction result.

## Success B: controlled extension

The accumulated vacuum principles allow:

```text
K_strain = K_EH/GHY + ε K_residual
```

with nonzero `ε`, and the residual satisfies conservation, boundary, stability, and observational weak-limit constraints.

Then the project becomes a candidate extension of GR.

The residual must be explicit enough to produce testable deviations.

## Success C: localized underdetermination

The candidate vacuum principles fail to determine a consistent local strain functional.

Then the project has proven that its current ontology is underconstrained and requires a new axiom.

This is not a vague failure. It identifies exactly where the missing axiom lives.

## Failure mode: performative reconstruction

The project should avoid writing more folders that merely prove:

```text
Assume the GR action; recover GR behavior.
```

That line is now sufficiently understood.

New work must either constrain `K_strain`, compute `ε`, prove a no-go, or explicitly propose a new axiom.
