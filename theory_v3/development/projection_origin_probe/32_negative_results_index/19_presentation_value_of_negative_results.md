# 19. Presentation Value of Negative Results

The negative results are among the most externally useful parts of the proof
chain because they delimit what cannot work:

```text
scalar boundary success does not scale to GR;
scalar nonlinear dressing cannot replace tensor geometry;
stress coupling cannot replace matter ontology;
finite flux cannot replace projection embedding;
r_k cannot replace EH/GHY assumptions.
```

## Closed result

Negative results should be presented as scientific constraints, not as failed
attempts. They explain why the project had to move from scalar admissibility to
metric/tensor/radiative geometry.
