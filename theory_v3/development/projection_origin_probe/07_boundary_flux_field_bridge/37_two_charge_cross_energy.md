# Radial Boundary Field Bridge 37: Two-Charge Cross Energy

## Purpose

This report validates the separation-dependent stored-strain part of the
Dirichlet energy for two exterior boundary charges using the 3D free-space
Green kernel.

The normalized kernel is:

```text
G(d) = 1/(4*pi*d).
```

## Validated Checks

- Green kernel harmonic off source: passed
- Green kernel unit flux: passed
- cross energy formula: passed
- inverse-square derivative: passed
- cross energy distance homogeneity: passed
- force distance homogeneity: passed

## Green Kernel Facts

SymPy verifies that:

```text
G(rho) = 1/(4*pi*rho)
```

is harmonic away from the source:

```text
Delta G = 0  for rho > 0,
```

and has unit flux:

```text
-4*pi*rho^2 G'(rho) = 1.
```

## Stored-Strain Cross Energy

For two charges `Q1` and `Q2` separated by distance `d`, Green's identity gives
the separation-dependent stored-strain cross term:

```text
E_strain,cross(d) = Q1*Q2*G(d)
                  = Q1*Q2/(4*pi*d).
```

The self-energy terms depend on source radii/cutoffs. They do not depend on the
separation `d` and therefore do not contribute to the force law.

## Separation Derivative

SymPy verifies the derivative of the stored-strain cross term:

```text
-dE_strain,cross/dd = Q1*Q2/(4*pi*d^2).
```

Thus the field strength generated by a boundary flux and the derivative of the
stored-strain cross term both have inverse-square scaling.

## Sign Bookkeeping

This is the positive stored-strain cross energy only. It is not yet the
physical reduced interaction energy.

For same-sign positive sources, treating this positive stored-strain term as
the full interaction gives the repulsive sign. The source-coupled reduced
action in proof `46` supplies the physical interaction bookkeeping:

```text
E_red,cross = -Q1*Q2/(4*pi*d).
```

## Interpretation

This proves the minimal Newtonian scaling bridge:

```text
Dirichlet field energy in 3D
  + boundary flux charges
  + Green identity
  -> stored-strain cross term proportional to 1/d
  -> separation derivative proportional to 1/d^2.
```

This does not yet prove a full gravitational field equation. It proves that the
boundary-flux interpretation lands on the correct first scaling law in the
spherically symmetric/exterior Green-kernel limit.
