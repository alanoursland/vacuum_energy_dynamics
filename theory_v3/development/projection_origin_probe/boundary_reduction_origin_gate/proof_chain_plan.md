# Boundary Reduction Origin Gate Proof Chain Plan

## Purpose

This proof group clarifies a recurring interpretive point:

the boundary is the analysis language of reduction, flux accounting, compactification, variation, and projection. It is not, by itself, the underlying physical ontology.

The original ratio

```text
r_k = (2k - 1)/(2k + 3)
```

is treated here as a standard boundary/admissibility artifact. It arises from moment-kernel cancellation, endpoint contact, integration by parts, and primitive boundary regularity. That does not mean the underlying physics is "the boundary". Rather, the boundary appears because bulk/local/interval physics is tested through surfaces, endpoints, compactified infinity, or admissible variational data.

## Strategy

The chain separates three levels:

1. **Bulk/local equation or response**: divergence laws, variational equations, and source equations live in the interior.
2. **Boundary reduction**: integration by parts, Gauss/Stokes flux, compactification, and endpoint contact produce boundary terms or surface invariants.
3. **Projection/admissibility artifact**: finite polynomial rows such as `psi_k` encode the admissible kernel of a boundary-reduced moment functional.

## Main claims to test

- Boundary terms are generated by integration by parts and variation of bulk energies.
- Conserved flux can be measured on boundaries without making the boundary the source ontology.
- Compactified endpoints are analysis representatives of infinity or support boundaries.
- The `r_k` ratio is a moment-kernel / endpoint-contact coefficient.
- The primitive identity producing `psi_k` is a boundary-regular integration-by-parts identity.
- The generalized ladder `r_(R,k)` is a standard endpoint-contact admissibility family.
- Changing the enclosing surface does not change the conserved charge when no source crosses the shell.
- Boundary data are ledgers, constraints, and reduction variables; they are not automatically new physical substances.

## Expected conclusion

The scalar projection ladder and its ratios should be understood as boundary-reduction/admissibility structure. The underlying physical origin still belongs to the larger relational interval/comparison and field dynamics. Boundary analysis exposes, constrains, and measures that dynamics; it does not replace it.
