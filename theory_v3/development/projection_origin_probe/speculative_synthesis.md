# Projection Origin Probe

## Formal structure, speculation pressure, and the boundary of interpretation

A markdown presentation synthesizing the uploaded derivation notes and the outside LLM reactions.

---

# The narrow question

The probe asks a smaller, killable question:

> Does the surviving projection hierarchy have a natural operator, source, boundary, or geometric origin, or is it only a formal artifact?

The correct standard of success is not “this resembles physics.”

The correct standard is:

> Can a concrete mathematical object force the observed hierarchy?

---

# The core object

The central projection form is

$$
A[k,j] = 2 \int_0^1 \psi_k(x)\,\phi_j(x)\,(1-x^2)^4\,dx
$$

with

$$
\phi_j(x)=x^{2j},
$$

$$
\psi_k(x)=x^{2k}-\frac{2k-1}{2k+3}x^{2k-2},
$$

and projection weight

$$
w(x)=(1-x^2)^4.
$$

Let

$$
a = 1-x^2.
$$

Then the weight is simply

$$
w=a^4.
$$

---

# The first warning: not an energy matrix

The row-test function can be written

$$
\psi_k(x)=x^{2k-2}(x^2-r_k),
$$

where

$$
r_k=\frac{2k-1}{2k+3}.
$$

So each row-test function changes sign at

$$
x_k=\sqrt{\frac{2k-1}{2k+3}}.
$$

That sign change kills the naive positive-Gram interpretation.

This hierarchy is not naturally read as a positive energy or Hessian matrix.

It behaves more like a constraint, compatibility filter, or admissibility projection.

---

# Speculative Observations

Several structural tensions were identified:

1. The sign-changing rows block a simple positive-energy interpretation.
2. The ratio looked like a product-rule or integration-by-parts residue.
3. The weight $a^4$ looked too structured to be arbitrary.
4. The two-term form of $\psi_k$ looked like an operator output, not a full orthogonal polynomial.
5. The source probes $S_{p,q}=x^{2q}(1-x^2)^p$ looked like endpoint diagnostics.

These observations are not all correct, but they are all testable.

# Diagnostics

## 1

The source family

$$
S_{p,q}(x)=x^{2q}(1-x^2)^p
$$

is not just a toy family. It probes the two endpoints separately:

```text
p: vanishing order at x = 1
q: vanishing order at x = 0
```

## 2

The provenance of the weight $a^4$ is more important than any later analogy.

---

# The first real result

The row-test function is generated by a boundary-vanishing primitive:

$$
G_k(x)=x^{2k-1}(1-x^2)^2.
$$

Direct differentiation gives

$$
\frac{d}{dx}\left[x^{2k-1}(1-x^2)^2\right]
=
-(2k+3)(1-x^2)\psi_k(x).
$$

Equivalently,

$$
\psi_k(x)
=
-\frac{1}{(2k+3)(1-x^2)}
\frac{d}{dx}\left[x^{2k-1}(1-x^2)^2\right].
$$

This derives the ratio

$$
\frac{2k-1}{2k+3}
$$

once the primitive power $m=2$ is chosen.

---

# What that result means

Before the derivative identity, $\psi_k$ was just a suspicious row function.

After the identity, $\psi_k$ has a concrete integration-by-parts origin.

This upgrades the hierarchy from

```text
unexplained projection rows
```

to

```text
boundary primitive + integration-by-parts pullback
```

But it does not yet assign physical meaning to $x$, $f$, or $S$.

---

# The pullback operator

Start with the pairing

$$
I_k[f]=\int_0^1 \psi_k(x) f(x) a^4\,dx.
$$

Using the primitive identity and integrating by parts produces

$$
\langle \psi_k,f\rangle_w
=
\frac{1}{2k+3}\langle x^{2k-1},L[f]\rangle_w,
$$

where

$$
L[f]=a f' - 6xf.
$$

Equivalently,

$$
L[f]=a^{-2}\frac{d}{dx}\left[a^3 f\right].
$$

So the projection is secretly testing odd moments of a transformed profile $L[f]$.

---

# The parity flip

The original basis is even:

$$
\phi_j(x)=x^{2j}.
$$

The integration-by-parts pullback converts the row test into odd moments:

$$
x^{2k-1}.
$$

That reveals hints of:

- first-order operators,
- folded coordinates,
- angular or parity-reduced structures,
- compatibility constraints rather than energy magnitudes.

This interpretation is suggestive, not decisive.

The verified statement is only:

> the even projection rows are equivalent to odd moment tests of $L[f]$.

---


---

# Source signatures as a diagnostic

For formal source probes

$$
S_{p,q}(x)=x^{2q}(1-x^2)^p,
$$

the sign structure is controlled, up to positive factors, by

$$
-(2kp + 6k - p - 4q - 3).
$$

The same coefficient $6$ that appears in

$$
L[f]=a f'-6xf
$$

also appears in the source-signature control expression.

That suggests the source diagnostics are governed by the same exponent structure as the IBP pullback.

This is not physical interpretation. It is a sharper way to use $S_{p,q}$ as an endpoint/domain probe.

---

# The operator has real structure

Under the weight

$$
w=a^4,
$$

the weighted adjoint is

$$
L_w^*[g] = -a g' + 4xg.
$$

The boundary term is

$$
[a^5fg]_0^1.
$$

For odd monomial tests $g=x^{2k-1}$, the boundary is clean at $x=0$.

For regular functions, it is clean at $x=1$.

So the pullback is not a trick; it is a coherent weighted-adjoint structure.

---

# The second-order compositions

The derived second-order operators are

$$
L_w^*L[f]
=
-a^2f''+12xa f' +(6-30x^2)f,
$$

and

$$
LL_w^*[g]
=
-a^2g''+12xa g' +(4-28x^2)g.
$$

These look like possible parent-operator candidates.

But the archive status is stricter:

> Keep them as tools unless a concrete variational, Sturm-Liouville, boundary-domain, or self-adjointness problem appears.

---

# The commutator clue

Subtracting the zeroth-order pieces gives

$$
(6-30x^2)-(4-28x^2)=2-2x^2=2a.
$$

So the operator pair has a clean commutator-like residue proportional to the base factor $a$.

This is the kind of thing that tempts ladder-operator interpretations.

That temptation is useful as a diagnostic.

It is not yet an external derivation.

---

# The artifact-risk test

The decisive stress test was to replace the observed primitive with a family:

$$
G_{k,m}(x)=x^{2k-1}a^m.
$$

Then differentiation gives

$$
G_{k,m}'
=-(2k+2m-1)a^{m-1}\psi_{k,m}(x),
$$

where

$$
\psi_{k,m}(x)
=
x^{2k}-\frac{2k-1}{2k+2m-1}x^{2k-2}.
$$

The observed hierarchy is the $m=2$ member because

$$
2k+2m-1=2k+3
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
m=2.
$$

---

# The family pullback

For the family, the pullback operator becomes

$$
L_m[f]=a f' - 2(5-m)xf.
$$

The observed operator is

$$
L_2[f]=a f'-6xf.
$$

For the regular range $1\le m\le 5$, the same integration-by-parts machinery works.

This is the main danger:

> The existence of an IBP construction does not uniquely select $m=2$.

So $m=2$ is real inside the observed hierarchy, but not yet independently explained.

---

# The exponent ladder

For general $m$, the exponents are

```text
primitive:   a^m
flux:        a^(5-m)
weight:      a^4
boundary:    a^5
```

For the observed case $m=2$, this becomes

```text
primitive:   a^2
flux:        a^3
weight:      a^4
boundary:    a^5
```

or

$$
a^2 \rightarrow a^3 \rightarrow a^4 \rightarrow a^5.
$$

This is the strongest remaining clue.

It is clean.

It is not yet a derivation.

---

# The generalized ladder insight

One useful operation was to generalize the ladder.

If the projection weight is

$$
w=a^\beta,
$$

then the analogous roles are

```text
primitive:   a^m
flux:        a^(β+1-m)
weight:      a^β
boundary:    a^(β+1)
```

Requiring a strict adjacent ordered ladder forces

$$
m=\frac{\beta}{2}.
$$

For $\beta=4$, this gives

$$
m=2.
$$

This is promising only if a separate principle first explains why the weight should be $a^4$ and why the ordered ladder is physically required.

---

# Selector test 1: adjoint closure

For the family,

$$
L_m^*[g] = -a g' + 2mxg.
$$

The adjoint relation is

$$
L_m^* = -L_{5-m}.
$$

This is real structure, but it is family-wide.

It pairs

```text
m = 1 ↔ m = 4
m = 2 ↔ m = 3
m = 3 ↔ m = 2
m = 4 ↔ m = 1
```

The fixed skew-adjoint point would be

$$
m=\frac{5}{2},
$$

not an integer primitive power.

Conclusion: adjoint closure does not select $m=2$.

---

# Selector test 2: compactified radial measure

The generic compactification test was

$$
r=\frac{x}{a^c},\quad c>0.
$$

An $n$-dimensional radial measure contributes the factor

$$
a^{-cn-1}.
$$

For positive $c$ and positive $n$, this is a negative power of $a$.

It cannot naturally equal the positive projection weight

$$
a^4.
$$

The standard case $c=1/2$ would formally require

$$
n=-10,
$$

which is not a positive dimension.

Conclusion: compactification alone does not explain the weight or select $m=2$.

---

# Structural Reading

The projection hierarchy has several features that should be treated as established diagnostic signals:

- the row functions are sign-changing, so they should not be read as positive-energy rows;
- the ratio $r_k = \frac{2k-1}{2k+3}$ is naturally explained by product-rule algebra rather than arbitrary fitting;
- the projection behaves more like an admissibility, compatibility, or balance condition than a magnitude measurement;
- the operator $L$ is best treated as a first-order flux-like object;
- the second-order composition $L_w^*L$ is a natural derived object to keep available, even if it is not yet a parent equation;
- the exponent ladder is the strongest internal clue in the construction.

The value of these observations is not that they settle the interpretation.

Their value is that they generate concrete tests.

# Interpretive Boundaries

Several readings are mathematically tempting but not yet earned:

- Jacobi or Gegenbauer structure may be relevant, but does not itself select the hierarchy.
- Associated Legendre analogies fit too many members of the primitive-power family.
- Radial compactification was explicitly killed as a standalone explanation.
- Thermodynamic or vacuum-response readings remain possible maps, not derived results.
- A variational energy functional would be powerful, but it must be independently supplied.
- The exponent ladder is suggestive, but it is not an independent derivation unless the ordering principle is justified.
- The observed $m=2$ case is structurally attractive, but the primitive-family test shows that the integration-by-parts mechanism is not unique to it.

The working rule is:

> resemblance is not derivation.

# Strongest speculative anchor: a variational object

The most useful speculative proposal is not “this resembles known physics.”

It is this:

> If a concrete energy functional produces $L_w^*L$ as its Euler-Lagrange operator, the projection hierarchy may become a Galerkin or moment projection of that variational problem.

A candidate shape would be

$$
E[f] = \frac12 \langle Lf, Lf \rangle_w - \langle S,f\rangle_w.
$$

Then the formal equilibrium equation would be

$$
L_w^*L f = S.
$$

---

# A correction to keep the deck clean

One analysis rewrote $L$ with the wrong rescaling sign.

The correct identity is

$$
L[f] = a^{-2}\frac{d}{dx}(a^3 f).
$$

Not

$$
a^4\frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{f}{a^3}\right).
$$

The useful instinct was right: $a^3 f$ is the natural flux-like grouped object.

The algebraic form must remain the validated divergence form.

---

# Variational Energy-Minimization Target

A possible external anchor for the hierarchy is an energy-minimization principle.

In this reading, the profile `f` is not selected merely because it satisfies formal moment conditions. It is selected because it is stationary, or possibly minimizing, relative to nearby admissible profiles:

```text
f -> f + εη
```

where `η` ranges over allowed comparison directions satisfying the same regularity and boundary conditions.

A natural candidate energy has the schematic form:

```text
E[f] = 1/2 <L[f], L[f]>_w - <S, f>_w
```

with:

```text
w = a^4
a = 1 - x^2
```

and:

```text
L[f] = a f' - 6xf
     = a^(-2) d/dx[a^3 f].
```

The first term measures weighted gradient or flux energy. The second term couples the profile to a source.

The stationarity condition is:

```text
δE[f;η] = 0
```

for every admissible variation `η`.

Formally,

```text
δE[f;η]
=
<L[f], L[η]>_w - <S, η>_w.
```

Using the weighted adjoint of `L`, and assuming the boundary terms vanish on the chosen domain, this becomes:

```text
δE[f;η]
=
<L_w^*L[f] - S, η>_w.
```

Thus the Euler-Lagrange equation would be:

```text
L_w^*L[f] = S.
```

This would give the operator composition `L_w^*L` a natural role as the second-order energy operator associated with the first-order flux operator `L`.

In that setting, the projection hierarchy could be interpreted as a finite-dimensional Galerkin or moment projection of the variational equation. The matrix would no longer be merely a formal projection matrix; it would be a stiffness-like matrix associated with comparing the energy of nearby profiles.

This is a stronger interpretation than the current formal result.

It would mean:

```text
not merely:
  f satisfies weighted moment identities

but:
  f is stationary relative to nearby admissible profiles
  under a weighted energy built from L[f].
```

However, this variational reading is not currently derived.

To promote it from a speculative target to a result, one would need an independent derivation of:

* the energy form `1/2 <L[f], L[f]>_w`;
* the weight `w = a^4`;
* the source pairing `<S, f>_w`;
* the allowed variation space;
* the boundary conditions that make the adjoint calculation clean;
* the reason this energy selects the observed operator `L`, rather than another member of the family `L_m`.

The zero-source case also needs care.

Since:

```text
L[f] = a^(-2) d/dx[a^3 f],
```

the condition:

```text
L[f] = 0
```

implies:

```text
d/dx[a^3 f] = 0
```

so:

```text
a^3 f = constant.
```

Thus:

```text
f = constant / a^3.
```

This is singular at `x = 1` unless the constant is zero. Therefore, under regular boundary conditions, the only zero-energy source-free profile is:

```text
f = 0.
```

So the variational picture does not by itself produce a nonzero trivial vacuum profile. A nonzero baseline profile would require either a different variable, a shifted energy, a constraint, or a separately specified background state.

The useful role of this variational target is therefore precise:

```text
It identifies what kind of external principle would close the formal gap.
```

If a first-principles argument independently produces this energy functional, then the hierarchy gains a natural parent problem:

```text
energy minimization
  -> Euler-Lagrange equation L_w^*L[f] = S
  -> projected moment/Galerkin hierarchy
  -> observed first-order pullback structure.
```

Until then, this remains a reopen condition, not an established interpretation.
