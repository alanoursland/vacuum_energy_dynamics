## The claim

If space is a packing, cosmic expansion has two possible readings —
a fixed graph whose cells stretch with a(t), or new cells created at
fixed scale. The two are forever indistinguishable to the metric
sector (identical volume history, identical dust stress). They are
nonetheless decidable TODAY, because they differ in their side
effects on locked quantities:

1. **Stretch drags G.** If the microphysical length that calibrates
   Newton's constant grows with a(t), then Ġ/G = −H ≈ −7×10⁻¹¹/yr.
   Lunar laser ranging bounds |Ġ/G| < 1.5×10⁻¹³/yr: excluded by
   ~480×.
2. **Stretch turns the vacuum's floor into dust.** Dilation-invariant
   wedge energies make the stretched floor's pressure exactly zero
   (w = 0) at ~Planck density, and a diluting density is not
   protected by the sequestering mechanism — overclosure by ~10¹²².
   A second, independent kill.
3. **Growth is exactly self-funding.** The intensive floor
   (numerically measured in the companion model) has p = −u: w = −1
   at the substance level, and the energy cost of each new cell
   equals the floor's negative-pressure work term by term. The first
   law funds expansion; no reservoir is needed.
4. **The surviving reading forbids G drift entirely.** Ġ = 0 —
   exactly, at every epoch — becomes a standing falsifier, and it is
   under genuine pressure: H₀ sits only ~480× above current LLR
   bounds, which tighten steadily. Distinctive against scalar-tensor
   and varying-G rivals, which generically need drift.
