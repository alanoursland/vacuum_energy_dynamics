## The claim

Three short theorems, each a page:

1. **Anisotropy lemma:** the higher-derivative part of the $R + aR^2$
   response carries an intrinsic t-r stress anisotropy
   ($D^t{}_t - D^r{}_r = -R''$), so any static scalaron profile defines
   a preferred frame in the t-r plane; equivalently, in areal gauge,
   $g_{tt}g_{rr} \neq -1$ wherever hair exists (the known f(R)-star
   property, sharpened to a frame statement).
2. **Mandatory-hair lemma:** fourth-order matching admits no hairless
   exterior for a sourced body ($x\cosh x = \sinh x$ has no positive
   root): for any $a \neq 0$, *every* static star leaks scalaron hair.
3. **Selection theorem:** therefore any principle that forbids preferred
   t-r frames in static vacuum — "static frame indifference," of which
   the Schwarzschild relation $g_{tt}g_{rr}=-1$ is the shadow — forces
   $a = 0$ exactly. Among $f(R)$ theories, frame indifference selects
   $f(R) = R$ (+ Λ).

Corollary with data: were the principle dropped, the bench-top Yukawa
limit (Lee 2020; α = 1/3 crossing at 54.05 μm — our vector-extracted
curve) caps the scalaron range; the principle's *null prediction* (no
gravitational-strength Yukawa at any range) is its cleanest test.

## Novelty check required (the gating question)

That f(R) stars have $g_{tt}g_{rr}\neq-1$ exteriors is known; that
chameleon/thin-shell papers discuss hair suppression is known. What we
have not found stated: (a) the no-positive-root mandatory-hair lemma in
this sharp form, (b) frame indifference *as a selection principle* with
the $a=0$ theorem. One careful literature pass (search: "f(R) exterior
solutions g_tt g_rr", "no-hair f(R) stars", Sotiriou–Faraoni reviews)
decides whether this is a letter or a paragraph of paper 1.
